THE
CITY OF
NEW YORK
OFFICE
OF THE
MAYOR
NEW YORK, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 185
November 26, 2012
MAYOR’S OFFICE FOR iNTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS
WHEREAS, the City of New York hosts the United Nations, 193 Permanent Missions to the United
Nations, and 114 Consulates; and
WHEREAS, it is essential to the City of New York to maintain excellent relations between the people of
the City of New York and the personnel of foreign governments and the United Nations located in New York
City; and
WHEREAS, in furtherance of this objective, the City’s Commission for the United Nations, Consular
Corps and Protocol has represented the City’s interests with regard to these foreign and international entities; and
WHEREAS, in recent decades the City has become home to the world’s largest diplomatic community
and is the preeminent location for international relations, commerce, culture and tourism; and
WHEREAS, the Commission is the City’s primary liaison to the international diplomatic community, and
it is appropriate for its name to reflect that fact;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City ofNew York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
The Commission for the United Nations, Consular Corps and protocol shall be known
henceforth as the Mayor’s Office for International Affairs.
§ 2.
The Commissioner of the Mayor’s Office of International Affairs shall be known as the
Commissioner for International Affairs.
§ 3. All references to the Commission on the United Nations, Consular Corps and Protocol in Executive
Order No. 53, dated November 21, 2001, and Executive Order No. 35, dated July 10, 1997, are hereby deemed to
refer to the Mayor’s Office of International Affairs. All references in those Orders to the Commissioner for the
United Nations, Consular Corps and Protocol are hereby deemed to refer to the Commissioner for International
Affairs.
§ 4. This Order shall take effect immediately.
Michael R. Bfoomberg
Mayor
