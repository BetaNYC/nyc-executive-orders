THE City oF NEw YORK
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE
ORDER NO. 28
MARCH 14, 1996
MAYOR’S COMMISSION
ON YOUTH EMPOWERMENT
SERVICES
WHEREAS,
the talent and vitality of the youth of the City are among the
Citys most valuable resources, and government funding for work experience programs for
young people has been significantly diminished; and
WHEREAS, identifying opportunities for quality work experience for young
people in the City is an effective method of developing their job-related skills and directing
their energies in ways that will inure to the benefit of the City and the Citys business and
not-for-profit communities; and
WHEREAS, providing access to private, public and not-for-profit sector jobs
will give young people an understanding of the job market so that they may make informed
choices regarding their futures; and
WHEREAS,
a focused and sustained coordination of public and private
resources to identify available jobs will ensure the widest possible choice of employment
experience for the Citys youth;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered that:

Section 1. Establishment of the Commission on Youth Empowerment Services.
The Commission on Youth Empowerment Services (the “Commission") is hereby
established. The Commission shall be comprised of members to be appointed by the Mayor,
including, but not limited to, members representing the business, labor, financial and
professional service communities, as well as the not-for-profit community. The Commission
shall also include, ex officio,
the Deputy Mayor for Education and Human Services, the
Commissioner of the Department of Employment and such other public officials as may be
appointed by the Mayor upon the recommendation of a majority of the Commission. The
Chancellor of the City School District of the City of New York shall be invited to participate
as a member. The Mayor shall designate a chairperson from among the members.
Members shall serve without compensation and at the pleasure of the Mayor.
Section 2. Powers and Duties. The Commission shall have the following
functions and responsibilities:
qa. Advise and assist the Mayor in a focused and coordinated effort to mobilize
both the public and private sector, including the for-profit and not-for-profit communites,
to identify existing employment opportunities, “to develop additional employment
opportunities that are appropriate for young people in the City, including summer jobs, parttime and full-time jobs and paid internships, and to develop methods to match the talents
and interestsof young people with available employment positions.
b. Advise and assist the Mayor in identifying funding sources, and develop
additional funding sources, for the purpose of funding a wide range of employment
opportunities for young people in the City.

c. Advis: and assist the Mayor in coordinating available resources of the
federal, stateand City governments and the private sector, including for-profit and notfor-profit entities, to provide funding, staffing, facilities and information to support the
implementation of programs developed by the Commission. The Commission also may draw
upon staff, in-kind contributions and support functions made available to the Commission
by private sector entities, including for-profit and not-for-profit entities, as well as public
sector entities.
d. Report to the Mayor regularly on the goals and achievements of the
Commission.
Section 3. Agency Assistance and Cooperation. All City agencies shall
cooperate in providing to the Commission personnel, facilities, information and other
assistance as may be necessary and appropriate to assist the Commission in carrying out its
responsibilities.
Section 4. EffectiveDate. This Order shall take effectimm diately.
“ Rudolph W. Giuliani
Mayor
