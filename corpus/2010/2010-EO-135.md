EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 135

June 30, 2010

EXTENSION OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK’S
EXTENDED MILITARY BENEFITS PROGRAM – DIFFERENTIAL PAY
PROGRAM

WHEREAS, many New York City employees in the military reserves have been
called to active duty since the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001;
WHEREAS, New York City’s Extended Military Benefits Program – Differential
Pay Program (the “EMBP-DP”) provides partial salary payments and continued benefits
coverage to City employees serving extended military deployments in connection with
certain designated operations;
WHEREAS, the EMBP-DP aids public servant soldiers who might otherwise be
forced to go on unpaid leave, and lose benefits coverage, to satisfy their military service
obligations;
WHEREAS, more than 1,900 City employees in the reserves have benefited from
the present EMBP-DP or its predecessor program during deployments frequently lasting
a year or more;
WHEREAS, the law of the State of New York requires that New York City
provide programs for public servant soldiers consistent with the City’s EMBP-DP but
only until August 1, 2010;

WHEREAS, paragraphs (c) and (d) of subdivision 5-a of section 242 of the
Military Law provide the Mayor with discretion to extend beyond August 1, 2010 the
salary and differential pay benefits provided to public servant soldiers pursuant to such
paragraphs; and

WHEREAS, it is critical that our men and women in the armed forces and their
families continue to receive such benefits;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:

Section 1. Pursuant to paragraphs (c) and (d) of subdivision 5-a of section 242 of
the Military Law, the rights and privileges granted therein to a public officer or employee
with respect to the payment of city salary and differential pay are hereby extended
beyond August 1, 2010, and shall continue unless otherwise specified by Executive
Order.
§ 2. This Order shall take effect immediately.

Michael R. Bloomberg
Mayor
