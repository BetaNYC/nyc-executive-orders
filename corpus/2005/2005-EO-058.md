EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 58
January 14, 2005
PROCLAMATION OF A STATE OF EMERGENCY

Whereas, Employees of Command Bus Company and Green Bus
Company, both of which companies have interim operating authority to provide service
in areas of the City where bus service is not provided by the New York City Transit
Authority, commenced a strike on January 10, 2005, and such strike has caused serious
disruption of the City’s transportation system; and

Whereas, It is necessary to insure public safety and the orderly
transportation of people and goods in the City of New York; and
Whereas, The strike could cause an increase in the response time of
emergency vehicles which will endanger the lives and health of the City’s residents,
commuters and visitors, inability to gain access to routine medical care, the loss of wages
for thousands of persons, the loss of revenue, on a daily basis, for businesses in the City,
and lost revenues for the City and the Board of Education, and significant increased
expenditures by the City of New York;
Whereas, These circumstances would constitute an imminent peril to the
public health and safety requiring the adoption of special rules, regulations, procedures
and restrictions as emergency measures;
Whereas, Executive Law §24 authorizes the Mayor to promulgate local
emergency orders upon a finding that the public safety is imperiled by the event of a
public emergency or in the event of reasonable apprehension of immediate danger
thereof, to protect life and property and to bring an emergency situation under control;
and
Whereas, Executive Order No. 2, dated January 1, 2002, authorizes the
Deputy Mayor for Operations to perform all the functions, powers or duties of the Mayor
which the Mayor is authorized to delegate in the event that the Mayor is unable to
perform such functions, powers or duties for a reason specified in subdivision a of section
10 of the New York City Charter;

Now, therefore, by the power vested in the Mayor of The City of New
York, and the power vested in the Deputy Mayor for Operations pursuant to Executive
Order No. 2, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. State of Emergency. A state of emergency is hereby declared
to exist within the City of New York.
§ 2. Commuter van and for-hire vehicle provisions. The following
provisions shall apply to vehicles licensed by the New York City Taxi and Limousine
Commission:

(a) All for-hire vehicles and commuter vans licensed as such by the Taxi and
Limousine Commission are authorized, for the duration of the emergency,
to pick up passengers without prearrangement at such locations within the
City as may be designated by the Chairperson of the Taxi and Limousine
Commission.

(b) All commuter vans licensed as such by the Taxi and Limousine
Commission are authorized to operate outside of the geographical area set
forth in their operation authorization, subject to geographic limits as may
be designated by the Chairperson of the Taxi and Limousine Commission,
and are further authorized to accept and discharge passengers on streets
within such geographic limits which otherwise constitute bus routes and at
points which otherwise constitute bus stops as approved by the
Chairperson.

§ 3. This executive order shall take effect immediately.
