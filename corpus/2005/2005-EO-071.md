EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 71
September 9, 2005
ENHANCING CITY CONTRACTING OPPORTUNITIES FOR MINORITY- AND
WOMEN-OWNED BUSINESSES AND OTHER SMALL BUSINESSES
WHEREAS, the City of New York has a diverse business community that
encompasses thousands of small businesses which generate substantial economic activity
and provide numerous employment opportunities throughout the City; and

WHEREAS, the City’s economic development and vitality are
inextricably linked to the continued growth and success of small and emerging
businesses; and

WHEREAS, many small businesses are disadvantaged in the market
because of the owners’ race or gender, or other factors in the owners’ social and
economic background; and

WHEREAS, the agencies of the City of New York enter into several
billion dollars’ worth of contracts each year to obtain construction, construction-related
services and other goods and services needed by the agencies for the exercise of their
powers, duties and responsibilities; and

WHEREAS,
recent
analysis
documents
statistically
significant
underutilization of minority- and women-owned businesses (collectively “M/WBEs”) as
City vendors, relative to their availability in the relevant market area and capacity to
supply the City’s needs; and

WHEREAS, the City must now take remedial steps to overcome factors
that have operated to exclude, inhibit or disadvantage M/WBEs and similarly situated
businesses pursuing opportunities to do business with the City; and

WHEREAS, the Charter provides that the Commissioner of Small
Business Services (“SBS Commissioner”) shall, “[e]xcept as otherwise provided by law,
… have charge and control of and be responsible for all functions and operations of the
city relating to business and economic development, [and] the enhancement of economic
development and financial opportunity for minority and women owned business
enterprises,” and authorizes the SBS Commissioner “to establish business, industrial and

commercial policies, programs and projects which affect the business, industrial,
commercial or economic well-being, development, growth and expansion of the
economic life of the city;… to study, organize, promote, coordinate and carry out within
or without the city, activities, projects and programs designed to encourage, stimulate and
foster the well-being, development, growth and expansion of business, industry and
commerce in the city, and the enhancement and protection of the economic life of the
city; … to promote and encourage the location and development of new business and
industry in the city, as well as the maintenance and expansion of existing business and
industry, and for this purpose to cooperate with public and private agencies, organizations
and individuals; [and] to maintain, operate and control such programs as may be
necessary or required to achieve the objectives of the department [of small business
services];” and

WHEREAS, the Charter provides for a Division of Economic and
Financial Opportunity within the Department of Small Business Services (“DSBS”), the
purpose of which is “to enhance the ability of minority and women owned business
enterprises to compete for City contracts, to enhance city agencies’ awareness of such
business enterprises, and to ensure their meaningful participation in the city procurement
process;” and

WHEREAS, the Charter authorizes the SBS Commissioner to promulgate
rules for these purposes;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of
New York, it is hereby ordered:

Section 1.
Program established. DSBS, in conjunction with the Mayor’s
Office of Contract Services (“MOCS”), and in consultation with the Procurement Policy
Board, shall promptly establish a program to enhance meaningful participation of
M/WBEs as prime contractors and subcontractors in City contracts. The program shall
be governed by this order and rules that shall be promulgated by the SBS Commissioner.
Such rules shall include, but not be limited to, provisions to:

(a)
establish criteria for certifying eligible businesses as M/WBEs;

(b)
implement a certification process that is not unduly burdensome to the eligible
businesses and that recognizes, to the maximum extent feasible, the
certification rosters maintained by other governmental entities operating
within the City’s market area;

(c)
maintain an ongoing directory of certified M/WBEs that shall be used by all
City agencies; and

(d)
establish standards and procedures which City agencies shall use in
promulgating annual procurement opportunity plans, incorporating M/WBE
participation goals for categories of their procurements, including but not

2

limited to prime contractor and subcontractor participation goals for
construction and construction-related services contracts and subcontracts at or
below one million dollars in value.

Such rules may also include provisions aimed at increasing opportunities for small
businesses whose owners have experienced social and economic disadvantage.

§ 2. Steering Committee. Prior to drafting such rules, the SBS Commissioner
and MOCS Director shall co-chair a steering committee consisting of the Director of the
Office of Operations, the Corporation Counsel and the Commissioners of the Department
of Design and Construction, Department of Transportation, Department of Housing
Preservation and Development, Human Resources Administration, Department of
Environmental Protection, Department of Citywide Administrative Services, Department
of Sanitation, Department of Parks and Recreation, Department of Information
Technology and Telecommunications, Administration for Children’s Services,
Department of Homeless Services, and the Department of Health and Mental Hygiene.
The committee shall:

(a)
seek guidance from various stakeholders, including principals of M/WBEs
and other small businesses who are City contractors or subcontractors and
principals of such businesses who have been unsuccessful in bidding for City
contracts, in executing subdivisions (b) through (d) of this section;

(b)
review and recommend program objectives and options for achieving such
objectives both through race and gender neutral and race and gender
conscious measures;

(c)
review the proposals of the Mayor’s Commission on Construction
Opportunity and recommend their incorporation into the rulemaking as
appropriate; and

(d)
recommend a process for ongoing monitoring and reporting of outcomes.

§ 3. Department of Small Business Services. In fulfilling the objectives of this
program, the SBS Commissioner shall:

(a)
design strategies, in a manner consistent with Section 2 of this Order, to give
opportunities to such businesses to participate as contractors and
subcontractors in appropriate contracts of the Department of Education, New
York City Housing Authority, Health and Hospitals Corporation, School
Construction Authority, and the Economic Development Corporation. The
head of each such entity shall cooperate with the SBS Commissioner in
providing detailed data and other assistance in the design of such strategies in
light of agency-specific procurement processes, and shall identify any

3

impediments to such data collection and tracking, so that they can be
addressed;

(b)
enhance existing programs and implement new programs, and market them
appropriately, to expand potential for both public and private sector
contracting opportunities for such businesses, including providing classes in
the contractor certification process as well as in capacity building, business
development, and mentoring programs, some of which may also be offered by
the private sector;

(c)
provide technical assistance for such businesses to enable them to obtain
contracts from the New York State Office of General Services (OGS), thereby
providing such businesses access to purchasing opportunities that City
agencies make from OGS contracts;

(d)
expand the scope and accessibility of existing directories of such businesses to
enable buyers in both the public and private sectors to identify and solicit
them for contracting opportunities;

(e)
enhance notification of bidding opportunities for such businesses through the
City’s bid-matching system, Softshare, as well as other channels;

(f)
enhance the process of debriefing unsuccessful bidders; and

(g)
maintain and consult with an M/WBE Advisory Board comprised of local
businesses and trade group partners.

§ 4. Mayor’s Office of Contract Services.

(a)
In fulfilling the objectives of the program with respect to M/WBEs, the
MOCS Director is hereby authorized to:

(1)
review all solicitations, other than invitations for competitive
sealed bids, prior to agencies’ issuance thereof, to ensure that any
requirements relating to potential vendors’ experience and qualifications
are reasonably related to the purpose of the procurement and will not
unduly restrict the opportunities of such businesses to compete for such
procurement;

(2)
direct agencies to set the threshold for the requirement of payment
and/or performance bonds by vendors at a level commensurate with the
City’s interest both in appropriate allocation of risks and in building the
capacity of small businesses to contribute to the City’s economic growth;

4

(3)
establish citywide protocols for the collection of all procurementrelated information necessary to monitor Mayoral agencies’ performance
of their obligations under this Executive Order, and in consultation with
the Commissioners of Information Technology and Telecommunications
and Citywide Administrative Services and the Director of the Office of
Management and Budget, ensure that the City’s financial and procurement
tracking systems are enhanced as soon as practicable, so that Mayoral
agencies have the capacity for automated tracking of bid/proposal pick-up
and response rates and the approvals of subcontractors, as well as to
permit vendors to enroll and submit procurement-related documentation to
the City on-line;

(4)
establish policies and procedures to facilitate agencies’ use of their
micropurchase authority to promote access by minority- and womenowned businesses and small businesses to City procurement opportunities,
including but not limited to the expansion of the City’s procurement card
program to include all Mayoral agencies, thereby facilitating such
agencies’ efficient purchase of goods, services, construction and
construction-related services;

(5)
review the curriculum of the City’s Procurement Training Institute
to recommend courses that should be added or modified, to ensure
appropriate training for agency procurement staff in policy and procedures
that promote access by such businesses to City procurement opportunities;

(6)
audit agency compliance with the provisions of this Executive
Order and with any rules promulgated thereunder on an ongoing basis, and
consider the results of such audits in determining the level of procurement
authority to be delegated to the ACCO of each Mayoral agency, pursuant
to Section 4 of Executive Order 48 of 2004;

(b) By no later than March 1, 2006, and biannually thereafter, the MOCS Director
shall issue report(s) analyzing the following areas of City procurement and making
recommendations
to

such

recommendations for proposed rules to be presented for adoption by the Procurement
Policy Board, as appropriate:

(1)
small purchases of goods, services, construction and constructionrelated services pursuant to Charter § 314;

(2)
requirements
contracts,
task
order
contracts
and
other
procurements wherein agencies have bundled or grouped procurements of
multiple goods, services, construction or construction-related services in
large-scale solicitations;

(3)
bid protest and contract dispute resolution procedures; and

(4)
payment procedures in connection with contracts for construction
and construction-related services, including but not limited to, payment of
mobilization costs, retainage at the conclusion of contracts, direct
payments to subcontractors and prompt payment of invoices;

(c)
The MOCS Director shall consult with the SBS Commissioner as he or she
deems appropriate in the implementation of the provisions of this section.

§ 5. Agency heads. Each agency head shall be responsible for implementing
an annual agency procurement opportunity plan, tracking agency performance under such
plan, and otherwise cooperating with the SBS Commissioner and the MOCS Director in
accordance with this order.
§ 6. This executive order shall take effect immediately.
