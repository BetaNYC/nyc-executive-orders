THE City oF NEW YorK
OFFICE OF THE Mayor
New York, N.Y. 10007
Executive Order No. 23
January 18
, 1991
HIV Health and Human Services
Planning Council
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section
1.
HIV
Health
and
Human
Services
Planning
Council Established.
There is hereby created an HIV Health and
Human
Services
Planning Council
of
the City of New
York
(the
"Planning Council").
Section 2.
Purpose.
The United States Congress enacted
the Ryan White Comprehensive AIDS Resources Emergency (C.A.R.E.)
Act of 1990 (the "Ryan White Act") to provide emergency assistance
to
localities
disproportionately
affected
by
the
HIV
epidemic.
Eligibility for grants under the program requires the establishment of
the
Planning
Council,
which
will
have
as
its
primary
duty
the
establishment of priorities for the allocation of funds the program will
make available for the development,
organization,
coordination and
operation of more effective and cost efficient systems for the delivery
of essential services to individuals and families with HIV disease.

Section 3.
Membership.
(a)
The Planning Council shall
be appointed by the Mayor and shall consist of not less than 35 and
not more than 42 members.
The members of the Planning Council
shall serve at the pleasure of the Mayor and from among them, the
Mayor shall designate a Chairperson.
(b)
The membership of the Planning Council shall include
representatives of health care providers, community-based and AIDS
service organizations,
social service providers,
mental health care
providers, local public health agencies, hospital planning agencies or
health
care
planning agencies,
affected
communities
(including
individuals with HIV disease), non-elected community leaders, State
government,
grantees of categorical grants
for early intervention
services under subpart II of part C of the Ryan White Act and the
lead agency of any Health Resources and Services Administration
adult and pediatric HIV-related care demonstration project operating
in the area to be served.
At least one member of the Planning
Council shall represent separately each of these specified categories.
(c)
The Mayor also
shall appoint one
member
that is
mutually acceptable
to
the
Mayor
and
the
County
Executives
of
Westchester,
Rockland
and
Putnam
Counties
to
represent
these
political subdivisions or Health Resources and Services Administration
grant recipients therein on the Planning Council.
(d)
The Planning Council shall also include as members the
Commissioner of Mental Health, Mental Retardation, and Alcoholism
Services of the City of New York, the Commissioner of Health of the
City of New York, the Administrator of the New York City Human

Resources Administration, the Director of the Office of Management
and Budget of the City of New York, and the President of the Health
and Hospital Corporation, or their designees.
Such designees must
be
at
least
an
Assistant
Commissioner
or
the
equivalent
of
the
agency.
(e)
The Mayor shall designate alternates for members of
the Planning Council who serve as individuals with HIV disease to
serve in the member's place if as a result of his or her illness the
member cannot perform the duties of a Planning Council member.
(f)
With respect to all members of the Planning Council
other than these enumerated in subsections (d) and (e), the Planning
Council may determine that such members may designate an alternate
to serve as a non-voting participant in the Planning Council on the
occasion of the member's absence.
(g)
There must be at least one member of the Planning
Council from each of New York City's boroughs.
(h)
The following shall serve as non-voting,
ex officio
representatives to the Planning Council:
Director of Mayor's Office
on Special Needs Housing, Director of Mayor's Office on Drug Abuse
Policy, Commissioner of Human Rights of the City of New York,
Commissioner of Aging of the City of New York, Director of Mayor's
Office of Operations, a representative from the Mayor's Office for
People with Disabilities, and a representative from the Mayor's Office
on Homelessness and SRO Housing.
(i)
In the event of a vacancy, the Planning Council may
within thirty (30) days after the vacancy occurs recommend to the

Mayor the names of three individuals who would satiafy the relevant
qualifying criteria to fill that vacancy.
Section 4.
Functions.
The Planning Council shall:
(a) perform the duties of a Planning Council as set forth in
the Ryan White Act, including the following:
(i) establish priorities for the allocation
of funds within the eligible area;
(ii) develop a comprehensive plan for
the organization and delivery of health
services described in Section 2604 of
the Ryan White Act that is compatible
with any existing State or local plan
regarding
the
provision
of
health
services
to
individuals
with
HIV
disease ;
(iii)
assess
the
efficiency
of
the
administrative
mechanism
in
rapidly
allocating funds to the areas of greatest
need within the eligible area; and
(b) perform such other functions and assume such other
additional responsibilities as the Mayor may from time to time propose.
Section
5.
Quorum
and
Voting
Requirements.
(a)
A
quorum of the Planning Council shall be fifty percent plus one person
of the Council's membership.
Once a quorum is present at any
meeting, the Planning Council may proceed to transact all business.
(b) Any act, determination or decision of the Planning
Council must receive a majority of the votes of those present and
voting at a meeting thereon in order to be held to be the act,
determination or decision of the Planning Council.
(c) An abstention from voting must be counted as a vote in
opposition for purposes of determining whether a majority of those
present and voting have approved an act, determination or decision of

Section 6.
Rules and Regulations.
The Planning Council
may adopt such by-laws and rules of procedure for the conduct of its
meetings or other activities as
it deems necessary to perform its
functions as enumerated in this Order.
Section 7.
Effective Date.
This Order shall take effect
immediately.
