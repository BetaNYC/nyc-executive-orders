EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 47
APRIL 1, 1980
——
TRANSPORTATION EMERGENCY
Whereas, Ali subway and bus services provided by the Metropolitan Transit Authority
have ceased operations ; and
Whereas, It is necessary to insure the orderly transportation of people and goods in The City
of New York; and
Whereas, I find that these circumstances constitute an imminent peril to the public health
and safety requiring the adoption of special rules and regulations as emergency measures;
4 Now theréfore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York it is hereby
ordered
;
Section 1.
State of Emergency. A state of emergency is hereby declared to exist with
respect to transportation within the City of New York.
§2.
Mahatian Trafic Restrictions.
All private passenger vehicles containing less than
2 persons are hereby prohibited from entering that portion of Manhattan situated south of
96th Street by any streets, highway bridge or tunnel.
§3.
Roadway Traffic Restrictions.
All private passenger vehicles containing less than
3 persons are hereby prohibited from entering and operating on the following roadways
during the hours of 6 a. m. to 10. a. m. Monday through Priday:
(a) Westbound Long Island Expressway, from the Queens-Nassau County boundary
to the Queens entrance to the Midtown Tunnel;
(b) Westbound Bruckner Expressway, from the Bruckner Interchange to the Triborough Bridge;
{c} Southbound Harlem River Drive, from Dykman Street to the FDR Drive at
125th Street ;
(d) Southbound FDR Drive, from 125th Street to 73d Street:
(e) Southbound Henry Hudson Parkway, from Moshola Parkway to 79th Street ;
({) Northbound Belt Parkway, from 4th Avenue to its merger with the Brooklyn-
Queens Expressway (60th Street);
.
‘(g) Eastbound Brooklyn-Queens Expressway, from 92d Street to Manhattan Bridge;
and
(h) Prospect Expressway northbound from Church Avenue to Gowanus Expressway.
§4.
Enforcement,
Provisions of this order shall be enforced as emergency measures
under Section 8a-9.0 of the Administrative Code and shall be punishable as provided therein
by a fine or imprisonment, or both, The Commissioner of Transportation
may adopt such
emergency rules and regulations as may be necessary to implement the provisions of this order
under Section 2%03{b) of the Charter; a violation of such rules and regulations shall be
unishable as provided therein by a fine or imprisonment or both.
