EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 63
February 18, 2021
ESTABLISHING THE CENTER FOR CREATIVE CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND
PROMOTING RESTORATIVE JUSTICE PRINCIPLES
WHEREAS one of government's central functions is to provide fair, transparent,
accessible, inclusive processes for resolving conflict within the New York City community and
between City government agencies and members of the community;
WHEREAS, the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings is already designated as
the City's independent impartial tribunal and resolves conflicts involving the City workforce and
the community through formal processes;
WHEREAS, workplace disputes can often be resolved through alternative dispute
resolution methods, without resort to costly litigation;
WHEREAS, disputes between City agencies and members of the communities they
serve can often be resolved with cost-effective alternative dispute resolution methods;
WHEREAS, conflict resolution and restorative approaches help to improve workplace
relationships, boost morale and increase productivity, thereby improving public service;
WHEREAS, punitive modes of enforcement tend to burden communities of color and
low-income communities disproportionately;
WHEREAS, the principles of restorative justice can in appropriate cases assist the City
in resolving disputes between residents, between residents and government agencies, and
between public agencies, in a manner that recognizes the dignity of the parties to the dispute,
respects the diverse backgrounds and interests of the parties, and seeks to find common ground
to develop mutually agreeable and beneficial solutions;
WHEREAS the City uses restorative justice efforts to build bridges and design initiatives
to improve relationships between City agencies and the communities they serve;

WHEREAS, the City seeks to consolidate leadership and coordination of conflict
resolution efforts taking place across City agencies, consistent with the powers and duties
assigned to City agencies;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it
is hereby ordered that:
Section 1. The Center for Creative Conflict Resolution ("the Center") is hereby continued
and formally established within the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings ("OATH"), to
serve as the City's central resource for Alternative Dispute Resolution ("ADR") processes and
conflict resolution services, including restorative justice practices.
The Center shall be led by an Executive Director whose functions include carrying out
the Center's duties and chairing the Center's Advisory Committee. The Executive Director shall
be appointed by the Chief Administrative Law Judge of OATH.
Section 2. Functions of the Center. The Center's responsibilities shall include, but not be
limited to:
(a) The provision of ADR and conflict resolution services, including services that
incorporate restorative justice practices, to all City agencies and City employees,
to address workplace conflicts;
(b) The provision of ADR and conflict resolution services, including services that
incorporate restorative justice practices, to City agencies, members of the public
and business establishments, to address conflicts between government and
members of the community, conflicts between public agencies, and conflicts
between and among members of the public and business establishments where
such conflicts affect City interests and concerns;
(c) The provision of training, strategic planning, technical assistance and other
support to City agencies, and their contractors, to facilitate their use of ADR and
conflict resolution services, including restorative justice practices, to enhance
public services, develop restorative justice programs and address conflicts in the
community; and
(d) The provision of restorative justice and community reconciliation programs to
provide support to criminal and civil law enforcement officers and agencies,
including support to such officers and agencies to facilitate communication with
other municipalities seeking to exchange information concerning best practices
regarding ADR, conflict resolution services and restorative justice practices.
Section 3. Duties of City Agencies.
(a) All agencies shall consult with the Center regarding restorative justice approaches
that can be incorporated into their workr consistent with applicable law.
(b) All agencies shall designate an ADR Coordinator to serve as a liaison to the
Center and to refer matters to the Center for services as appropriate.

Section 4. Establishment of an Advisory Committee. There is hereby established under
the direction of the Center an Advisory Committee.
(a) The Advisory Committee will convene regularly.
(b) The Advisory Committee will be chaired by the Center's Executive Director, and
will have no fewer than seven members, including the Chair, with other
appointees being members of the public with relevant expertise in any of the
following areas: ADR, restorative justice, ADR scholarship, conflict resolution,
organizational leadership and development, change management, dispute system
design, online dispute resolution and group conflict management. Members will
be appointed by the Chief Administrative Law Judge of OATH, in consultation
with the Center's Executive Director.
