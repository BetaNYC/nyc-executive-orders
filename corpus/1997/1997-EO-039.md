EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 39
REPORTING OF NEW ADDRESSES, STREETS, AND OTHER LOCATIONS TO
EMERGENCY RESPONSE AGENCIES
September G
, 1997
WHEREAS, the Police Department, the Fire Department, and the Emergency
Medical Service (the "Emergency Response Agencies") maintain computerized databases of
addresses and other locations within the City of New York for purposes of enabling such
agencies to respond to emergencies that occur within the City of New York; and
WHEREAS, it is imperative that the computerized databases on which Emergency
Response Agencies rely in locating emergencies to which they respond be comprehensive and
accurate; and
WHEREAS, the Borough Presidents have agreed to assist in the process of
updating the Emergency Response Agencies’ computerized databases of addresses and other
locations by timely transmitting to such agencies information concerning the construction of new
developments and/or streets and official address changes and new house numbers within their
respective boroughs;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:

Section 1.
Definitions:
"Mapped Street": a street that is contained in the current city map referred to in
section 198 of the New York City Charter.
"Unmapped Street": a street that is not contained in the current city map referred
to in section 198 of the New York City Charter.
§2. The Department of Buildings shall require that an applicant for the following
permits and certificates certify that such applicant has provided the Emergency Response
Agencies and the Department of City Planning with a site plan that shows the nearest Mapped
Streets that are open and in use and shows the access route from such Mapped Streets to the
subject building or location:
a.
a building permit relating to a new building on either (i) a subdivided
zoning lot or (ii) a zoning lot that fronts a private street, an Unmapped
Street, or
a Mapped Street that is not open;
an alteration permit relating to the alteration of an existing building on
either (i) a subdivided zoning lot or (ii) a zoning lot that fronts a private
street, an Unmapped Street, or a Mapped Street that is not open;
a foundation permit, excavation permit, and/or land contour permit
relating to work on either (i) a subdivided zoning lot or (ii) a zoning lot
that fronts a private street, an Unmapped Street, or a Mapped Street that
is not open; and
a temporary certificaté of occupancy, or a permanent certificate of
occupancy if no temporary certificate of occupancy was obtained, relating
to a building on either (i) a subdivided zoning lot or (ii) a zoning lot that
-2-

fronts a private street, an Unmapped Street, or a Mapped Street that is not
open.
§3. Within five (5) business days of receipt of the information from an applicant
referred to in Section 2 hereof, the Emergency Response Agencies shall ensure that the location
identified by such information appears in their computer databases of addresses and other
locations.
$4. The Department of Transportation shall require that an applicant for a permit
relating to a street that does not appear in the Department of Transportation’s database of street
names certify that such applicant has provided the Emergency Response Agencies and the
Department of City Planning with a site plan that includes the nearest Mapped Streets that are
open and in use and shows the access route from such Mapped Streets to the subject street.
§5. Within five (5) business days of receipt of the information from an applicant
referred to in Section 4 hereof, the Emergency Response Agencies shall ensure that the location
identified by such information appears in their computer databases of addresses and other
locations.
$6. The Department of City Planning will immediately make available to the
Emergency Response Agencies each release of its Geosupport System through such system’s
installation at the computer service center of the Department of Information Technology and
Telecommunications.
§7. The Emergency Response Agencies and the Department of City Planning
each shall advise the Department of Transportation and the Departmént of Buildings of the
name, office, address, telephone number, and facsimile transmission number of the person to
whom the information required to be transmitted by this Order is to be transmitted.
