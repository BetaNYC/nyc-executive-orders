EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 35
July 10, 1997
THE COMMISSION FOR THE UNITED NATIONS,
THE CONSULAR CORPS AND PROTOCOL
WHEREAS, it is essential to the City of New York that excellent relations be
maintained between the personnel of the United Nations and the various foreign consulates and
the people of the City of New York; and
WHEREAS, in furtherance of this objective the City provides to the personnel of
the United Nations and the Consular Corps information about City services, as well as other
forms of guidance, assistance and referrals;
WHEREAS, there exists a need
to provide a sensitive response to matters of
protocol and to maintain an economical and effective operating structure to plan, coordinate and
direct official receptions and ceremonies;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
There is hereby established in the Office of the Mayor a Commission
for the United Nations, the Consular Corps and Protocol.
Section 2. The Commission shall be headed by a Commissioner for the United
Nations, the Consular Corps and Protocol. The Commissioner shall serve without salary and
at the pleasure of the Mayor, and be the Mayor’s personal representative.
Section 3. The Commissioner shall have the following duties:
a. Promoting the role of the City of New York as the headquarters of the United
Nations, and for this and related purposes, maintaining close liaison with the United Nations
Secretariat and the individual delegates and United Nations personnel.
b. Promoting the role of the City of New York as one of the main centers of
Foreign Consulates, maintaining close liaison with the Consular Corps.

c. Acting for the Mayor as official ambassador to heads of state, distinguished
visitors, and other dignitaries.
d. Acting as special advisor to the Mayor on all matters involving the ceremonial
forms and courtesies in the official City greeting of heads of state and distinguished visitors.
e. Representing the Mayor at designated receptions and ceremonies.
f. Maintaining liaison and coordination with the United States Department of State
and the United States Mission to the United Nations with regard to issues related to the United
Nations, the Consular Corps, and official and unofficial visits to the City of New York by heads
of state and other dignitaries.
g. Informing United Nations and Consular Corps officials of services available
to them from the various City agencies and assisting in making those services available.
h. Assisting the diplomatic community to become familiar with and obtain access
to services available in the City, especially in the areas of education, health, hospitals, and
housing, in both the public and private sectors.
i. Working with voluntary neighborhood and community organizations on matters
related to the United Nations and the Consular Corps.
j. Working with the Consular Corps and the international business community to
assist in the enhancement of the City’s role in international trade and commerce.
k. Maintaining contact with the Office of Special Projects and Community Events
in connection with the scheduling of all official visits, and related operational requirements.
|. Participating in the planning of itineraries and programs for foreign visitors.
m. Establishing guidelines on matters of protocol.
n. Directing, planning, and coordinating all activities of the Commission.
Section 4. The following Executive Orders are hereby superseded: Executive
Order No. 6, dated April 8, 1994; Executive Order No. 76, dated April 30, 1984; and Executive
Order No. 7, dated April 10, 1970.
Section 5. This Order shal! take effet immediately.
