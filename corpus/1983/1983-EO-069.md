EXECUTIVE ORDER NO.
69
September 28,

PROHIBITION OF THE CONDUCT OF CITY
BUSINESS AT PRIVATE CLUBS THAT ENGAGE
IN DISCRIMINATORY MEMBERSHIP PRACTICES
Section 1.
Policy,
It has been a fundamental policy of the City of
New York to act to combat discrimination on account of race, creed,
color, national origin, sex, age, marital status, sexual orientation,
affectional preferences, or disability.
Section 2.
Purpose.
To prohibit the conduct of city business at
private clubs, or at the facilities of private clubs, which restrict,
limit or bar membership, or the use of dining or recreational facilities
in violation of the policy set forth above.
City business activities
will not support entities engaged in discriminatory practices which
constitute an affront to the public at large and work to the social and
economic disadvantage of those who are discriminated against,
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of
New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 3.
Prohibition,
No city business shall be conducted at and
no city funds shall be expended in connection with any meeting held at a
private club, or any facility of a private club, which does not afford
full membership rights and privileges or accord use of dining and recreation
facilities, to any person because of race, creed, color, national origin,
sex, age, marital ‘status, sexual orientation, affectional preference, or
disability.
Section 4,
Effective Date,
This Order shall take effect immediately,
