Establishment of the Inter-Agency Health Council
Whereas, It is in the best interest of the people of The City of New York that health
care policy and the delivery of health services be closely coordinated among the various departments and agencies providing those services; and
_ Whereas, There exists the historical precedent of the Interdepartorental Health Council
which was established to coordinate health services and functicned from 1952 to 1967.
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it is
hereby ordered that:
Section 1.
Creeton—There is established the Inter-Agency Health Council for
The City of New York.
§2.
Functions, Powers and Duties—-The general purpose of the Inter-Agency
Health Council is to provide maximum coordination of health services for the people
of this City, and without limiting the foregoing, it is empowered and directed to:
a.
formulate city policy for the delivery of health-related services requiring
inter-agency coordination.
b.
develop inter-agency approaches to specific issues.
c.
contribute to the resolution of problems caused by overlapping jurisdictions.
d.
stimulate the evaluation of inter-agency coordinated programs and activities
in light of emerging needs and priorities.
e.
exchange information and foster staff cooperation among the agencies represented in the Inter-Agency Health Council.
f.
seek funding from outside sources to assist in any of the above.

§4.
Chairman—The Commissioner of Health shall be the Chairman of the Inter-
Agency Health Council for the first full calendar year. The chairmanship will thereafter rotate among the membership in accordance with rules the Council adopts.
