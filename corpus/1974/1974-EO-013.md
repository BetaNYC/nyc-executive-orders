ESTABLISHMENT OF THE
MANHATTAN CIVIC CENTER TASK FORCE
_ Whereas, The preponderance of The City of New York’s executive legislative and
judicial functions are concentrated in the City Hall area of Manhattan, and
Whereas, The City Hall ot New York ts a unique structure of immense architectural,
historical and cultural importance which must be
preserved in its present physical form
and setting as a iasting reminder of the great traditions of this City and its people, and
hereas, The volume of the City’s governmental activities has grown over the years
to the point where the existing facilities in City Hall and nearby municipal buildings unduly
restricts the expeditious and efficient performance of these responsibilities, and
Whereas, This presents a clear and urgent need for a modern City Hall Annex and
other facilities in the Manhattan Civic Center area to permit improvement in the conduct of
essential governmental functions, and
Whereas, Many complex plans and designs for a modern and efficient Manhattan Civic
Center have been formulated in the past, but never brought to fruition, and
Whereas, In addition to servicing the needs of the government of the City of New
York, the Manhattan Civic Center area serves as the governmental center for the offices and
agencies of the Federal and State governments located within the City of New York, and
Whereas, A need exists to insure the proper planning, design, acquisition, construction,
development and coordination of the Civic Center and to maintain a close liaison and cooperation with all interested persons and groups, both public and private, including representatives of State and Federa) governments and community groups and organizations, and
Whereas, In addition, the Manhattan Civic Center area will also serve as a focal point
for the many events being planned for the City’s bicentennial anniversary celebration in 1976.
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor, it is hereby ordered as follows:
