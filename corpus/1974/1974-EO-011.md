EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 11
a,
March 14, 1974
—-
ESTABLISHMENT OF THE CRIMINAL JUSTICE
COORDINATING COUNCIL
Whereas, There are numerous public and private agencies involved in the City’s criminal justice system,
Whereas, There is a need to coordinate the efforts of these separate agencies,
Whereas, The Federal Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act requires that local
criminal justice agencies develop coordinated pians and programs,
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it is
hereby ordered as tollows:
.
:
Section 1,
There is hereby continued in the Office of the Mayor, a Criminal Justice
Coordinating Council, which shall plan and coordinate crime contro! and criminal justice
activities for the City.
§2.
The Mayor shall appoint a Director of the Council, who shall serve at the pleasure
of the Mayor. He shall serve as Chairman of the Council,
§3.
The duties of the Director shall include but not be limited to the following:
(a) to confer with appropriate City, State, Federal, and private agencies concerned with the administration of criminal justice for the purpose of improving crime
control programs and policies ;
(b) to confer with appropriate City, State, Federal, and private agencies for the
purpose of securing funds for the support of the Council and for initiating programs
of crime control and criminal justice reform, and, on behalf of the City, to accept, and
enter into contracts for, grants of Federal, State or other funds to the City for such
purposes;
{c) to advise the criminal justice agencies on improved policies and progrants;
(d) to conduct research, operate programs, and conduct studies of crime control
justice; and to contract with other public or private agencies and engage consultants
for such research programs and studies;
(e) to prepare and publish such reports and sponsor such conferences as he deems
appropriate ;
(f) to encourage joint activities among the separate criminal justice agencies and
to represent the overall interest and needs of the criminal justice system:
(g) to collect statistics and information relative to the crimitial justice agencies,
to design information systems for the standardization and collection of data and to
repare and publish reports, statistics and analyses of criminal justice operations,
Ba. (a) The Mayor shall appoint the members of the Council to serve at his pleasure.
(b) The membership of the Council shall include representatives from all public
agencies substantially involved in the criminal justice system.
(c) The Council shall have no less than twenty and no more than thirty-five members.
(d) The members of the Council shall serve without compensation,
§5.
The Mayor shall appoint an Executive Committee of not more than seventeen
members which shall act on behalf of the Council.
._
The Executive Committee: (a) is hereby designated to act as the planning agency
for the City under the provisions of the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act, and
(b) shall have the power to review and approve applications on behalf of the City for Federal
and State crime contro! and criminal justice funds.
§7.
All City agencies shall furnish the Director with such reports and information
as he may deem necessary to carry
out the functions and purposes of his office.
§8.
The Director shall submit an annual report to the Mayor and the members of
the Council,
§9.
This Order shail be effective immediately.
ABRAHAM D. BEAME, Mayor.
