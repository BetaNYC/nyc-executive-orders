EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 147
April 25, 2011
DEPUTY MAYORS
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. There shall be six Deputy Mayors. One shall be designated the First Deputy
Mayor, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Legal Affairs, one shall be designated the
Deputy Mayor for Economic Development, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for
Operations, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Government Affairs and
Communications, and one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Health and Human
Services. All Deputy Mayors shall collaborate with one another to ensure well coordinated
policies, operations, and administration of government.
§ 2. The First Deputy Mayor shall:
a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Cultural Affairs, the
Department of Parks and Recreation, the Department of Design and Construction, the
Department of Consumer Affairs, the Commission for the United Nations, Consular Corps and
Protocol, the Commission on Women’s Issues, Office of Veterans’ Affairs, and all
administrations, departments, agencies, boards, offices, committees and commissions except as
otherwise provided herein, provided that the Department of Education, Law Department, the
Police Department, the Fire Department, the Office of Emergency Management, the Department

of Investigation, the Office of Management and Budget, the Office of Labor Relations, and the
Office of the Criminal Justice Coordinator shall report directly to the Mayor.
b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Mayor’s Offices of Administrative
Services, Appointments, Scheduling and Advance, Correspondence, Special Projects and
Community Events, NYC Service, and Citywide Event Coordination and Management and
supervise City sponsored public events.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Public Design Commission the
Landmarks Preservation Commission, the Mayor’s Fund to Advance New York City, the Gracie
Mansion Conservancy, the Central Park Conservancy, the Governors Island Corporation, the
Brooklyn Bridge Park Corporation, libraries, museums, performing arts organizations, gardens
and zoos.
d. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor, including authorizations and
approvals to be made or given by the Mayor pursuant to sections 384, 824 and 1802 of the
Charter, in relation to the authorization or execution of deeds and satisfactions, leases,
easements, surrenders, restrictive declarations, mortgages and any other grants or acquisitions of
estates or interests in real property and licenses, permits or other agreements affecting the use of
real property.
f. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 3. The First Deputy Mayor hereby is delegated the authority to perform all functions,
powers or duties of the Mayor which the Mayor is authorized to delegate in the event that the

Mayor is unable to perform such functions, powers or duties for a reason specified in subdivision
a of section 10 of the New York City Charter.
§ 4. The Deputy Mayor for Legal Affairs shall:
a. Provide counsel to the Mayor on legal matters.
b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Office of the Administrative Justice
Coordinator, the Commission on Human Rights, the Office to Combat Domestic Violence, the
Office for People with Disabilities, and the Office of Immigrant Affairs.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Mayor's Committee on the
Judiciary, the Conflicts of Interest Board, the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings, the
Environmental Control Board, the Civilian Complaint Review Board, the Commission to
Combat Police Corruption, and the Equal Employment Practices Commission.
d. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 5. The Deputy Mayor for Economic Development shall:
a. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning, and programs designed to
foster the economic development or growth of the City, including those relating to the City's
overall business climate, the attraction, retention and expansion of domestic and global business
enterprises in the City, efforts to secure unique events, commercial, industrial and residential
development, waterfront development, energy, foreign trade and investment, government
regulation, film, theater and broadcasting, entertainment and sports facility development,
infrastructure, tourism and air, land and sea freight.

b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of City Planning and the
Office of Media and Entertainment.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the City Planning Commission, New
York City Housing Authority, the Housing Development Corporation, the United Nations
Development Corporation, the Board of Standards and Appeals, NYC & Company, and the Rent
Guidelines Board.
d. Serve as Chairperson of the Commercial Incentive Boundary Area Commission and
the Industrial and Commercial Incentive Board.
e. Supervise and coordinate the activities of the Department of Small Business Services,
the Department of Housing Preservation and Development, Department of Transportation, the
Business Integrity Commission, the Mayor’s Office of Pensions and Investments, and the
Economic Development Corporation as provided in the Amended and Restated Contracts
between the City and the Economic Development Corporation, and take such actions thereunder
as are reserved to the Deputy Mayor.
f. Maintain liaison with the New York State Department of Economic Development, the
City Empowerment Zone, the Empire State Development Corporation and its subsidiaries,
including the Lower Manhattan Development Corporation, the Port Authority of New York and
New Jersey, NYC and Company and other governmental and non-governmental agencies and
offices with respect to matters of City economic development and programs.
g. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor, including authorizations and
approvals to be made or given by the Mayor pursuant to sections 384, 824 and 1802 of the
Charter, in relation to the authorization or execution of deeds and satisfactions, leases,
easements, surrenders, restrictive declarations, mortgages and any other grants or acquisitions of

h. Collaborate with the Deputy Mayor for Operations to advise and assist the Mayor
regarding tax and pension policies.
i. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to any contracts with or
involving the New York City Industrial Development Agency or any other entity created for the
purpose of issuing bonds for, or managing, any project relating to any matter described in this

Transitional Authority Act, as the same may from time to time be amended, including, but not

k. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to the execution or
authorization of such franchises, concessions and revocable consents and other agreements as
relate to the duties of the Deputy Mayor for Economic Development.
l. Perform any function, power or duty which the Mayor has and is authorized to delegate

Code section 5-304.

§ 6. The Deputy Mayor for Operations shall:
a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Buildings, Department of
Citywide Administrative Services, Department of Environmental Protection, Department of
Finance, Department of Information Technology and Telecommunication, Department of
Sanitation, Department of Records and Information Services, and the Offices of Contract
Services, Operations, Environmental Coordination, Environmental Remediation, Long Term
Planning and Sustainability, and 311.
b. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Taxi and Limousine Commission
and the Tax Commission, the Tax Appeals Tribunal and the Loft Board.
c. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning and programs related to
the management of the agencies that report directly to the Mayor, except for the Department of
Education, Department of Investigation and Law Department.

d. Develop, supervise and coordinate policies and programs relating to personnel and
management practices.
e. Direct and coordinate the activities of all administrations, departments, agencies,
boards, offices, committees and commissions with respect to energy policies, planning and
programs and shared services.
f. Collaborate with the Deputy Mayor for Economic Development to advise and assist the
Mayor regarding tax and pension policies.
g. Act for and on behalf of the Mayor in the exercise of all functions, powers and duties
which the Mayor may have pursuant to the New York State Local Finance Law, as the same may
from time to time be amended, the New York State Financial Emergency Act for the City of
New York, as the same may from time to time be amended, Article 10 of the Public Authorities

of the New York State Environmental Conservation Law or the rules and regulations of the State
Department of Environmental Conservation, and to sign any other documents as may be
necessary to secure such funds.
i. Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the City, official statements of the
City relating to the issuance of the City's obligations.

effectuate any of the matters referred to in this section or any of the programs, projects and

o. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
p. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 7. The Deputy Mayor for Government Relations and Communications shall:

8

a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Office of Intergovernmental Affairs, the
Office of City Legislative Affairs, the Office of State Legislative Affairs, and the Office of
Federal Affairs.
b. Maintain liaison with the Board of Elections and the Campaign Finance Board.
c. Serve as the Mayor's representative, either directly or through the Offices of City and
State Legislative Affairs, on, and maintain liaison with, regional bodies and intergovernmental
agencies operating within the New York metropolitan region.
d. Maintain liaison, either directly or through the Office of Federal Affairs with the
United States Conference of Mayors, the National League of Cities, the New York Conference of
Mayors and other similar bodies.
e. Develop and implement the Mayor’s city, state and federal legislative agenda, serve as
the Mayor’s primary liaison to city, state and federal legislators and staff, supervise and
coordinate the legislative agenda of Mayoral agencies and develop, supervise and coordinate
policies and programs to secure funds from the federal and state governments.
f. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
g. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
