EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 49
ESTABLISHMENT OF TRANSIT PASS PROGRAM
July 7, 2000
WHEREAS, Federal law authorizes employers to offer their employees the
opportunity to elect to use pre-tax earnings to purchase transit passes for use on mass transit
facilities; and
WHEREAS, the Office of Labor Relations recently negotiated a citywide
collective bargaining agreement with District Council 37 of the American Federation of State.
County and Municipal Employees that offers approximately 160,000 City employees the
opportunity to elect to use pre-tax earnings to purchase Metrocards; and
WHEREAS, establishment of a Transit Pass Program that offers City employees
who are not represented in collective bargaining the opportunity to elect to use pre-tax earnings
to purchase Metrocards would reduce the cost of mass transportation for employees who
participate in the Program, thereby making the use of mass transportation by such employees for
commuting to and from work and for other purposes a more attractive option; and
WHEREAS, an increase in the use of mass transportation by City employees
would reduce automobile use, thereby decreasing automobile emissions in the City as well as
wear on City streets;

ae
WHEREAS, City employees who are not represented in collective bargaining
should be offered the opportunity to participate in a Transit Pass Program, both to enjoy the cost
savings and facilitate the environmental benefits associated with a Transit Pass Program, as well
as to achieve equality with the approximately 160,000 City employees who have been offered
the opportunity to elect to use pre-tax earnings to purchase Metrocards;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. Definitions:
“City employees” means elected officials of the City of New York and employees
of such officials, including employees of mayoral agencies, provided that such official's or
employee’s pay is processed by the Office of Payroll Administration, and provided, further, that
the term “City employees” shall not include employees who are represented in collective
bargaining.
“Transit Pass Program” or “Program” means the program established in
accordance with this Order and other applicable law under which City employees are offered the
opportunity to elect to use pre-tax earnings to purchase Metrocards.
§ 2. Transit Pass Program:
The Office of Payroll Administration. under the direction of the Mayor’s Office
of Operations, shall hereby establish and administer a Transit Pass Program.
§ 3. Program Guidelines and Procedures:
The Office of Payroll Administration shall develop appropriate guidelines and
procedures to establish and administer the Transit Pass Program including, without limitation,
guidelines and procedures that:
bo

a. establish the procedure by which City employees may elect to participate in the
Program;
b.
establish the amount of earnings that may be used by City employees to
purchase Metrocards;
c. establish the commencement date of the Program;
d. establish a fee, if necessary, to be charged to City employees who participate
in the Program to cover the cost of establishing and administering the Program, and
e. establish a cost-effective and secure method for multi-point distribution of
Metrocards.
§ 4. This Order shall take effect immediately.
