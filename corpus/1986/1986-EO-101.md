VOTER ASSISTANCE PROGRAM
By virtue of the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of
New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Voter Assistance Program.
(a)
The New York City Voter Assistance Program is hereby
established to provide access to voter registration forms for
persons who utilize the services of New York City agencies and
institutions.

(b)

Section 2.
City Agencies.
(a)
This section shall apply to the following city agencies,
and such other agencies as may be designated by the Voter
Assistance Coordinator:
ee
Department of Consumer Affairs
Department of Employment
Department of Health
Department of Housing Preservation and Development

(b)
During the hours and in the places in which it is open
to the public, each agency subject to this section shall, at
locations where
it is suitable and practical to do so, make mail

registration forms available to persons who wish to register to
vote.
The Commissioner of each such agency shall appoint a
liaison to ensure agency compliance with the Voter Assistance
Program.
Each such agency shall also designate employees who
shall be available to assist the public in filling out the mail
registration forms.
If an agency determines that it cannot make
space or employees available for the Voter Assistance Program, the
agency shall so inform the Voter Assistance Coordinator, and shall
prominently display the address of the nearest office where mail
registration forms are available.
(c}
Employee assistance under this section shall include
explanation of the mail registration process and the questions
contained on the mail registration form.
Employees shall maintain
a position of strict neutrality with respect to a person's party
enrollment.
{d)
No employee shall accept a completed mail registration
form.
The agency shall prominently display the proper address to
which such forms may be mailed.
{e)
The Voter Assistance Coordinator shall ensure sufficient
distribution of mail registration forms to agencies subject to
this section, and shall provide for instruction of designated
employees regarding the Voter Assistance Program.
Section 3.
Cooperative Efforts.
The Voter Assistance Coordinator shall attempt to enlist the
cooperation of other public agencies, not-for-profit institutions
and private firms in operating programs similar to the Voter
Assistance Program.
Such agencies, institutions and firms shall
include but not be limited to the following:
Brooklyn Public Library
New York Public Library
Queens Borough Public Library
City University of New York
Board of Education
Health and Hospitals Corporation
New York Housing Authority
community boards
banks
department stores
Section 4.
Other Duties of the Voter Assistance Coordinator.
(a)
The Voter Assistance Coordinator shall solicit comments
and proposals from the public or interested entities concerning
the Program.

(b)
The Voter AsSistance Coordinator shall report to the
Mayor on or by December 31st of each year on the progress of the
Voter Assistance Program, including its operations at city
agencies and its cooperative efforts.
The report shall include
suggestions for the improved administration of the Program.
Section S$.
Effective Date.
This Order shall be effective thirty days after it is issued.
