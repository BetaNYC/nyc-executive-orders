MAYOR'S COMMITTEE ON APPOINTMENTS
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Committee Established.
The Mayor's
Committee on Appointments (hereinafter called the Committee)
is hereby established in the Office of the Mayor to recruit,
to evaluate, to consider and to nominate candidates fully
qualified for appointment and to evaluate incumbent members
for reappointment to the following commissions and boards:
art commission, board of health (other than the chair),
board of standards and appeals, city planning commission
(other than the chair), civil service commission,
landmarks
preservation commission, tax commission, taxi and Limousine
commission and the public members of the environmental
control board, and any other commission, board or public
body as shall be determined by the Mayor.
Section 2.
Functions.
The Committee shall:
(a)
Recruit for consideration persons appearing to
have the highest qualifications for the offices referred to
in section one, receive recommendations of persons for
consideration as provided by law, and accept applications
from persons seeking appointment to such offices;
(b)
Evaluate and conduct all necessary inquiry to
identify those persons whose character, ability, training,
experience temperament and commitment to public service
fully qualify them for such offices;
(c)
Refer to the Department of Investigation for
screening all persons the Committee proposes to noniinate for
appointment;

(d)
Nominate three or more candidates for appointment
to each vacancy arising each in such office and submit to
the Mayor the names of the nominees and such information as
may be necessary to inform the Mayor of the qualifications
of each nominee; provided that when the Committee have
received recommendations of persons for consideration as
provided by law, the Committee may, where appropriate,
nominate such persons or report its recommendation against
the appointment of such persons to the Mayor; and provided
further that if there is more than one vacancy on any
commission, board or other public body the Committee may
submit fewer than three nominations for each vacancy unless
the Mayor specifically requests three nominations; and
(e)
Evaluate the qualifications of each incumbent in
such offices for reappointment to such office and report the
Committee's recommendation to the Mayor, provided that if
the Committee shall recommend against reappointment it shall
nominate three or more candidates for appointment to the
vacancy as provided above.
Section ¥.
Appointments by the Mayor.
(a)
The Mayor shall not appoint initially any person
to an office referred to in section one unless such person
is nominated, or reappoint an incumbent to any such office
unless such incumbent is recommended for reappointment, by
the Committee.
(b)
Within ninety days of a vacancy occurring in such
office the Mayor shall submit the name of the nominee to
fill such vacancy for the advice and consent of the Council
pursuant to section forty-six of the Charter, unless a
longer period is required in the public interest.
The
Committee may begin the process of considering nominees for
a vacancy before the term of the incumbent expires, and
should act expeditiously to assure that nominations are
timely made.
(c)
After the nominee to such office is confirmed by
the Council, the remaining nominations shall be valid for
appointment to any vacancy occurring in that commission,
board or other public body within six months after their
submission to the Mayor; provided, however, that the
Committee may nominate additional persons to fill any such
vacancy, and may renominate persons for any such vacancy
eccurring more than six months after their prior submission.

Section 4.
Committee Membership.
(a)
The Committee shall consist of five members
including a chair, each residing or having a principal place
of business in the City of New York, appointed by the Mayor.
Such members shall designate adjunct members who shall
function under section two as voting members with regard to
appointments and reappointments to specific commissions,
boards or other public bodies.
Adjunct members will be
professionals or knowledgeable persons of high
standing with expertise appropriate for enabling the
Committee to make a reasoned, informed and impartial
evaluation of candidates for the offices referred to in
section one.
There shall be between two and four adjunct
members for each commission, board or other public body for
which candidates are evaluated, according to the
determination of the members.
(b)
Members shall serve terms of two years commencing
January 1, 1986.
Adjunct members shall function under
section two as voting members until the appointments or
reappointments for which they have been designated are
confirmed and may be redesignated at the discretion of the
full members.
Vacancies in the Committee shall be filled in
the same manner as initial appointments.
A member of the
Committee may by removed by the Mayor for cause.
(c)
The Committee shall be selected with due
consideration for broad community representation.
The
membership shall include men and women and members of
minority and other groups, who are qualified to perform the
functions of the Committee.
(d)
No person shall be considered by the Committee for
any position covered by this order while serving as a member
of the Committee or within one year thereafter.
Section 5.
Confidentiality.
All Committee
communications concerning the qualifications of candidates
shall be made and held in confidence except such
communications as the Committee may submit to the Mayor or
as may be otherwise necessary and proper to the conduct of
Committee proceedings.
Section 6.
Committee Action and Procedure.
The
Committee may adopt such procedures and policies as it may
deem appropriate to its functions, including standards for

evaluating the best qualified candidates for nomination and
criteria for recommending the reappointment of incumbents.
Section 7.
Financing and Staff.
(a)
Members of the Committee shall receive no
compensation for their service as members,
(b)
The Committee shall be provided with paid staff
and sufficient facilities to carry out its functions.
(c)
Members of the Committee shall be reimbursed for
necessary expenses incurred in connection with the
responsibilities of the Committee.
Section 8.
Effective Date.
This order shall take
effect immediately.
