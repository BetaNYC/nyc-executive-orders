OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 33
AUGUST 4, 1975
ENFORCEMENT OF WORK RULES AND REPLACEMENT OF
EMPLOYEES TERMINATED BY DISCIPLINARY ACTION
Whereas, The overwhelming majority of the City’s employees are competent, dedicated,
‘|
hard-working men and wotnen, a small number of troublesome or incompetent employees
‘| create discipline problems and endanger the morale, productivity, and reputation of their
conscientious and
productive associates; and
Whereas, It is difficult to accept the fact that many of the hard-working, efficient employees must be laid off during this time of fiscal crisis while some unproductive workers
remain on the
payroll; and
:
Whereas,
It is the responsibility of all Administrators, Commissioners and Heads of
Agencies and their subordinates to ensure that employees under their jurisdiction report
to work on time, put in a full day's work, and leave only at the end of their work day unless
otherwise authorized; and
Whereas, It is the responsibility of the Vacancy Control Board to maintain stringent
controls over the filling of positions; and
Whereas, The replacement of troublesome or incompetent employees would increase
productivity without increasing costs.
Now, Therefore, by virtue of the powers vested in me as Mayor of The City of New
York, I hereby direct all Administrators, Commissioners,
Agency Heads and subordinates
to unequivocally enforce all applicable work rules and to
discharge their responsibilities
to the fullest extent by taking disciplinary action and exacting appropriate penalties for
any infringement of said work rules.
.
.
It is hereby ordered that appropriate agency personne! execute such agency-wide orders
as ate necessary to reaffirm the policies contained in this Executive Order.
It is further ordered that the Vacancy Control Board take such steps as are necessary
. to allow the replacement of any employee terminated solely as a result of disciplinary action
or due to an unsatisfactory probationary period.
This Order shall become effective immediately.
ad-35
ABRAHAM D. BEAME, Mayor.
