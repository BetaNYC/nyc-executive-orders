OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 27
MARCH 21, 1975
Establishing the Commission for Cultural Affairs of The City of New York
Whereas, The City of New York has been long renowned as an international center
of cultural resources and facilities for the enjoyment and education of all of its citizens
and the countless visitors who, each year, share these rich experiences ; and
Whereas, The arts have for countless years flourished and brought fame and excitement to our City ; and
Whereas, All the people of this City bask in the beauty and glory of our vast cultural
capacity ; and
Whereas, The continuous maintenance and growth of the arts is a continuing concern,
so that the blessings and benefits of these cultural resources may be made available to
enhance and enrich the lives of all of the people of this City: and
Whereas, The general welfare of the people of New York City will be promoted and
enriched by the continued recognition of the notable contributions of the arts and the
further study of means and programs to expand our cultural activities and maintain our
heritage in these areas.
Now, therefore, by
the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it
is hereby ordered as follows:
Section 1.
Executive Order No. 70, dated March 19, 1968 is hereby repealed.
