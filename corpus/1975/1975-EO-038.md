EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 38
AUGUST 29, 19775
ELIMINATION OF THE OFFICE OF CIVIL DEFENSE
. Whereas, The City of New York is making every effort to achieve cost-saving economies; an
Whereas, It is necessary to eliminate the Office of Civil Defense and reorganize the
. function as the Office of Emergency Services—Civil Preparedness; and
Whereas, The Office will be established under the Police Commissioner pending the
completion of the one year review and analysis of City emergency response mechanism
by the Mayor’s Task Force on Emergency Preparedness; and
Whereas, Substantial economies will be achieved by the transfer of the functions to
._ the Police Department ; and
Whereas, The Defense Civil Preparedness Agency at the Federal, State and tocal
level are working with the City to enable the reorganization to meet federal standards and
comply with legislative requirements; and
Whereas, The City, under this reorganization, will continue to be eligible for reimbursement under the federal
government’s matching funds program; and
Whereas, The Mayor’s
Emergency Control Board, established with the Mayor as
Chairman, the First Deputy Mayor as Deputy Chairman and consisting of Administrators
of Municipal Services Administration, Transportation Administration, Environmental
Protection Administration ; Commissioners of Fire, Health, Police, Public Works, Traffic,
Sanitation, Water Resources, Social Services; President, Health and Hospitals Corporation; Chairman, Metropolitan Transportation Authority, shall continue in order to meet
City emergencies, The Board will coordinate its activities with the Office of Emergency
Services in planning for natural or man-made disasters,
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of Nw York,
it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Executive Order No. 51 dated September 11, 1967 is hereby repealed.
§2.
The Police Commissioner is hereby appointed as the interim coordinator of the
functions of Emergency Services—Civil Preparedness, pending completion of a review
and analysis of the emergency response mechanism of The City of New York.
The Mayor’s Emergency Control Board will continue to be responsible for
coordinating activities in case of natural or man-made disaster.
:
§4.
The Director of Emergency Services—Civil Preparedness shall be responsible
to the Police Commissioner and shall serve as the local Director of Civil Defense, with
the powers of a local Director of Civil Defense under L. 1951, Ch. 784, as amended.
§5.
The functions of the Office of Emergency Services—Civil Preparedness shal!
include:
(a) Coordination of New York City’s Civil Defense effort in accordance with
the provisions of the Defense Emergency Act of New York State and development of
a Civil Defense Emergency operations plan involving all relevant municipal agencies;
(b) Preparation of plans designed to contain and contro! emergency conditions
caused by a disaster, maintain essential services during a disaster and to restore
essential services ;
(c) Aiding various City departments in obtaining funds under the Federal Government Matching Funds Program for Civil Defense activities;
(d) Carrying out all Civil Defense functions mandated by the Defense Emerency Act of New York State that are not transferred to other City departments
by this Executive Order.
§6.
The Municipal Broadcasting System shall continue to be responsible for communications functions of the former office.
§7.
The Department of Public Works shall continue to be responsible for the
Shelter Management functions of the former office.
§8.
The Police Department shall continue to:
(a) Maintain the Borough and Emergency Control Centers on a standby basis.
(b) Develop plans for activating these centers in an actual emergency.
§9.
The Fire Department shall continue to be in complete charge of Fire Auxiliaries
and keep the necessary records of these units. The Police Department shal! continue to be in
complete charge of the Police Auxiliaries and the Rescue Service and keep the necessary
records of these units,
§10.
The Commissioner of Public Works shall continue to have the primary responsibility for the coordination of all City and private sector equipment, such as trucks, generators, bull-dozers and power shovels. Public Works will continue to be responsible for
the replacement and maintenance of the City’s 742 sirens.
§11.
The Health and Hospital Departments shall continue their civil defetse assignments.
§12.
The Fire Department shall continue to maintain and operate a radiological
detection and reporting system.
:
Alt municipal agencies with civil defense responsibilities not specifically mentioned in this Executive Order, shall continue these responsibilities.
§ 14.
This Order shall take effect immediately.

ABRAHAM D. BEAME. Mayor.
