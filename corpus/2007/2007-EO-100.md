EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 100

March 16, 2007

 COORDINATION OF CITYWIDE SPECIAL EVENTS
AND PERMITTED ACTIVITIES

WHEREAS, New York City is world-renowned for hosting a variety street
activities and other events requiring the issuance of permits by the City, including
gatherings in City parks, processions, parades, street fairs, block parties, commercial
street events, displays, filming for motion pictures and television, and movie and theater
promotional events that enhance the City’s image internationally and generate tourism
and community and economic development throughout the City; and

WHEREAS, the authority for the issuance of permits, and the review and
implementation of such special events and other permitted activities is divided among a
variety of agencies; and

WHEREAS, coordination to enhance communication and cooperation among the
various agencies involved in the issuance of permits, or reviewing and implementing
such special events and other permitted activities would further tourism and community
and economic development in the City, and ensure that affected agencies are provided
with timely information for proper advance planning so that unnecessary disruptions to
vehicular and pedestrian traffic, and excessive noise in neighborhoods hosting events, are
avoided; and

WHEREAS, coordination and enhanced communication and cooperation
regarding these activities would advance public safety and improve overall service to
individuals and organizations seeking to organize street events and other activities
requiring the issuance of permits;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered that:

§ 1.
There is established in the Office of the Mayor the position of Coordinator
of Citywide Special Events and Permitted Activities. With regard to the permitting,
review and coordination of special events and other permitted activities, the Coordinator
shall, after consultation with affected agencies, and to the extent not inconsistent with
law:

(a) Serve as a repository and clearinghouse of information about
upcoming and ongoing special events and other permitted
activities, including but not limited to gatherings in City parks,
processions, parades, street fairs, block parties, commercial street
events, displays, filming for motion pictures and television, and
movie and theater promotional events;

(b) Provide necessary information in order to plan for and otherwise
discuss upcoming and ongoing street events and other permitted
activities, and to ensure informed decision-making by affected
agencies with regard to their issuance of permits or other decision
making.

(e) Regularly meet individually and in committee with affected
agencies in order to plan for and otherwise discuss upcoming and
ongoing street events and other permitted activities, as well to
discuss policies, plans, procedures and operations common to the
permitting, review and coordination of street events and other
permitted activities;

(f) Advise and assist the Mayor in the coordination of policies, plans,
procedures and operations common to the permitting, review and
coordination of street events and other permitted activities;

(g) Work with affected agencies and offices of elected officials, in
consultation with the Mayor’s Office of Intergovernmental Affairs,
to improve service and outreach to citizens and organizations
seeking to organize street events and other permitted activities;

(h) Work with affected agencies to improve the uses of technology
involved in the permitting, review and coordination of street events
and other permitted activities, including but not limited to, the
creation of a potential satellite uplink mechanism for productions
filming in Times Square and other locations, the development of a
common internal information system to track all upcoming and
ongoing events and other activities, the development of a single

website to facilitate the permitting of street events and other

(i) Provide affected agencies with timely information for proper
advance planning to ensure that unnecessary disruptions to
vehicular and pedestrian traffic, and excessive noise in
neighborhoods hosting events, are avoided;

(j) Issue a set of guidelines, within forty-five days of the date of this
Order, to be followed by affected agencies to facilitate the
implementation of the provisions of this section;

(k) Perform such other duties as may be assigned.

§ 2.
Affected agencies shall provide such assistance and cooperation as
deemed appropriate by the Coordinator.

§ 3.
For purposes of Sections 1 and 2 of this Order, the term “affected
agencies” shall include, but is not limited to, the following agencies: the Mayor's Office
of Film, Theatre and Broadcasting, the Department of Buildings, the Department of
Parks, the Department of Sanitation, the Department of Transportation, the Mayor’s
Community Assistance Unit, the Department of Citywide Administrative Services, the
Police Department, the Fire Department, the Department of Consumer Affairs, the
Department of Health and Mental Hygiene, the Department of Information Technology
and Telecommunications, and NYC & Company.

§ 4.
For purposes of Sections 1 and 2 of this Order, the term “street events and
other permitted activities” shall include, but not be limited to, gatherings in city parks,
processions, parades, races, street fairs, block parties, commercial street events, displays,
filming for motion pictures and television, and movie and theater promotional events.

§ 5.
The Coordinator shall report to the First Deputy Mayor.

§ 6. This Order shall take effect immediately, and shall be filed with the City
Clerk who shall forward it to the City Record for publication. The inclusion of NYC &
Company as among the affected agencies shall be subject to the concurrence of the Chief
Executive Officer of that entity.
