EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 109
ENERGY SAVINGS AND REDUCTION OF GREENHOUSE GAS EMISSIONS IN CITY
BUILDINGS AND OPERATIONS
October 22, 2007
WHEREAS, providing municipal services and fulfilling the obligations and duties of
New York City government require the use of energy resources; and
WHEREAS, the generation and use of energy have a significant impact on the
environment, including the emission of greenhouse gases that contribute to global warming; and
WHEREAS, under PlaNYC, New York City has a goal of reducing its Citywide
greenhouse gas footprint by 30% by 2030; and
WHEREAS, City government consumes more than 6% of all energy used in New York
City, but has succeeded in stabilizing its greenhouse gas emissions through the use of energyconscious policies such as retrofitting buildings to make them more energy efficient and
installing LED traffic lights; and
WHEREAS, in PlaNYC and the Mayor’s FY2008 executive budget, the City has
expressed its commitment to reduce energy consumption by City government, committed 10% of
its FY2008 energy expenses to fund energy-saving investments in City buildings and operations,
and proposed committing that amount each year going forward; and

WHEREAS, it is imperative for each City agency to significantly reduce its energy use to
realize this Citywide mandate; and

NOW, THEREFORE, by virtue of the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:

Section 1. Carbon Reduction Goal. The City shall implement cost-effective programs
and energy-efficient practices designed to reduce the City government’s energy consumption and
CO2e emissions by 30% from 2006 levels, as measured by the Inventory of New York City
Greenhouse Gas Emissions of April 2007, within ten years of the effective date of this Executive
Order. All agency heads are responsible for reducing energy consumption in the facilities that
they manage.

§ 2. Energy Conservation Steering Committee. An Energy Conservation Steering
Committee (“Steering Committee”) shall be established, consisting of representatives of the
Office of the Deputy Mayor for Administration, the Department of Citywide Administrative
Services, the Mayor’s Office of Operations, the Mayor’s Office of Management and Budget
(“OMB”), the Department of Design and Construction, and the New York City Economic
Development Corporation, to oversee and manage the development and implementation of the
City’s plan to reduce its carbon emissions. The Steering Committee shall be chaired by the
Deputy Mayor for Administration or his designee, and represented agencies shall provide staff
support. The Steering Committee shall also advise City agencies on all uses of funds allocated to
carbon-reduction efforts in City government.

§ 3. Energy Reduction Plans. a. By December 1, 2007, the Steering Committee shall
issue a short-term action plan for the use of the $80,000,000 budgeted in FY2008 for energy and
greenhouse gas emission reduction purposes. Projects that reduce the City’s energy consumption
and/or greenhouse gas footprint will be considered plan-eligible, including but not limited to
building retrofits, process and plug-load improvements, methane capture projects, and vehicle
retrofits or replacements. All buildings for which the City pays energy costs shall be considered
plan-eligible, and all forms of energy consumed, whether purchased centrally or not, shall be
considered plan-eligible. Return on investment and overall energy savings shall be the primary
bases for selecting and prioritizing projects. The Steering Committee shall allocate
approximately 30% of the budgeted allocation for FY2008 to assist in the planning and
management of energy-saving efforts in the future, such as additional staffing needs,
benchmarking studies, training efforts, and management systems.
b. By June 30, 2008, the Steering Committee shall issue a long-term action plan for the
achievement of the full 30% reduction in the City’s energy consumption and CO2 emissions by
2017. To the extent not already addressed by the short-term action plan to be established
pursuant to subdivision (a), the plan shall address:

i. Benchmarking of the energy consumption of all City buildings
(and any building for which the City pays energy costs),
reporting CO2 emissions and all types of energy use on a per
square foot or other unit basis over time;

ii. Identification of existing resources to be integrated into this
plan, including contract resources and information systems
(such as the Asset Information Management Survey);

iii. Identification of energy savings opportunities, including
establishment of an energy audit program to rank potential
sources of savings in buildings, and assessment of the
opportunity to utilize more efficient vehicles in the City’s fleet
and to reduce the overall use of non-emergency response
vehicles by City employees;

2

iv. Timelines to procure and implement program resources,
including but not limited to programs such as operations and
maintenance, building retrofits, real-time metering and
monitoring, water conservation, central management systems,
training of City facilities managers and others in energy-saving
maintenance, co-generation and standard specifications for
small-scale renovations;

v.
Measurement and verification protocols that will track
program savings;

vi. Pilot projects to assess the feasibility and economic potential
of new energy-saving technologies and methods;

vii. A structure through which agencies that manage facilities
will identify their own opportunities to reduce energy
consumption in an ongoing manner and contribute those
opportunities to an inter-agency plan for carbon reduction;

viii. Accountability and enforcement policies that will ensure that
agency heads undertake the energy savings for which they
are responsible;

ix. Any
staffing
or
other
organizational
needs
that
implementation of the plan may require; and

x. Any other energy-related projects that may contribute to the
goal of reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

§ 4. Implementation. City agencies shall provide all necessary assistance to members of
the Steering Committee for the purpose of developing and implementing the short-term and
long-term plans.

§ 5. This Order shall take effect immediately.
