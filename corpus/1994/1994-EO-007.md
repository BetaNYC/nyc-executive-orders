THE City oF NEw YORK
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New YorRK, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 7
April 12, 1994
Special Counsel to the Mayor for the Fiscal Oversight of Education
WHEREAS,
City Charter § 106(c) provides that "(t]he mayor shall keep
informed, during the course of each fiscal year, of the progress of expenditures and the receipt
of revenues, and it shall be the duty of all agencies, when requested by the mayor, to supply all
‘nformation needed for this purpose", and this section of the Charter applies to the fiscal and
budgetary administration of the Board of Education; and
WHEREAS, City Charter § 803(a) authorizes the Mayor to direct the
Commissioner of the Department of Investigation to make any investigation, and subdivision
(b) of that section authorizes the Commissioner of the Department of Investigation to". . .make
any study or investigation which in his opinion may be in the best interests of the city, including

but not limited to investigations of the affairs, functions, accounts, methods, personnel or
efficiency of any agency", and the exercise of these powers by the Commissioner of the
Department of Investigation applies to the Board of Education; and
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Special Counsel to the Mayor for the Fiscal Oversight of Education.
There is hereby created the position of Special Counsel to the Mayor for the Fiscal Oversight
of Education (hereafter "Special Counsel"), whose duty it shall be to oversee the budgetary
procedures and finances of the Board of Education of the City of New York and of the overall
City School District of the City of New York. The Special Counsel shall be a Special Deputy
Commissioner of the Department of Investigation, and shall receive no compensation, other than
reimbursement for actual and necessary expenses incurred in carrying out the duties of the
position.
Section 2.
Powers and Duties.
(a) The Special Counsel shall issue periodic
reports to the Mayor on the budgetary procedures and finances of the Board of Education of the
City of New York and of the overall City School District of the City of New York, including
an accurate accounting of the allocation of resources and personnel of the City School District
of the City of New York.

(b) In carrying out his or her duties, the Special Counsel shall obtain from the
;
City School District of the City of New York, including the Board of Education, the Chancellor,
the Community School Boards, the Community Superintendent, and any other officer or
employee of the school district, all information and data, including computer records, necessary
to prepare the reports provided for in subdivision (a) of this section. The Special Counsel is
authorized to consult with and obtain the assistance of
foundations, graduate schools of
education and other knowledgeable persons or entities in exercising his or her functions, and
may retain independent consultants to provide assistance in the performance of his or her duties.
(c) In the exercise of the duties set forth herein, the Special Counsel shall review
and analyze the relevant records, information, data bases, reports, plans, projections and any
other relevant materials of the City School District of the City of New York,
(d) The Department of Investigation, the Office of Management and Budget and
other Mayoral agencies shall provide to the Special Counsel such personnel, facilities and other
assistance necessary to enable the Special Counsel to carry out his or her responsibilities.
Section 3. Effective Date. This
er shall take effect immediately.
