_ WHEREAS, on September 6, 1966 the City of New York and the Legal Aid
Society entered into an agreement for the provision of representation of indigent defendants in
criminal proceedings (“Agreement”); and
WHEREAS, on October 4, 1994 the City terminated the Agreement; and the
Legal Aid Society subsequently questioned such termination; and
WHEREAS, Paragraph Eleventh of the Agreement provides that the Agreement
“may be terminated by the City, acting by executive order of the Mayor, ... by service by
certified mail of a notice of termination effective ninety (90) days after such service;” and
WHEREAS, I have determined that it is in the best interest of the City to exercise
its right under Paragraph Eleventh of the Agreement to give notice, and to use the ninety day
period to negotiate with the Legal Aid Society, with the purpose of reaching agreement upon
modifications to the Agreement that will ensure the continuous provision of constitutionally
d will contain important fiscal,
mandated services to indigent criminal defendants, an
programmatic and professional responsibility protections for the City that are lacking in the
current Agreement; and
WHEREAS, it is my intention to revoke the termination of the Agreement and
fo rescind this Notice if the City and the Legal Aid Society reach agreement upon such
modifications;
