EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 5
APRIL 7, 1994
NEW YORK CITY EMPOWERMENT ZONE
WHEREAS, Subchapter C of Title XIII of the Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act
of 1993, Public Law 103-66 (the "Act"), authorizes the establishment of an Empowerment
Zone Program under which communities of not more than 200,000 people may be
designated to receive special investment and training credits and special funding for social
services to enhance the economic self-sufficiency of residents of the zone; and
WHEREAS, the Act authorizes local and state governments, or economic
development corporations chartered by the states, to nominate areas for designation as
Empowerment Zones; and
WHEREAS, the communities of Harlem and the South Bronx, comprising the areas
to be specified further in the application for Empowerment Zone designation, contain
numerous residents in need of special investment and training credits and special funding
for social services to enhance their economic self-sufficiency, and otherwise meet the
eligibility criteria for designation as an Empowerment Zone under the Act; and
WHEREAS, the City of New York and the State of New York have determined to
nominate the Harlem and South Bronx communities for designation as an Empowerment
Zone under the Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act; and
WHEREAS, the Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act mandates that the designated
community must be a full partner in the strategic planning process leading to submission of
an application for designation of Empowerment Zone status; and
WHEREAS, the participation of various City agencies in the strategic planning
process is necessary to the preparation of an application for designation of the Harlem and
South Bronx communities as an Empowerment Zone;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York
it is hereby ordered as follows:

Section 1.
Strategic Planning and Application Process.
a.
The nominated communities of Harlem and the South
Bronx shall be made full partners in the strategic planning process for the application for
Empowerment Zone status, and New York City’s officials, coordinated by the Executive
Director of the Economic Policy and Marketing Group under the direction of the Deputy
Mayor for Finance and Economic Development, will act appropriately to insure that this
condition is met.
b.
New York City shall act to ensure that the strategic
planning process reflects the views of residents of the Zone, representatives of the business
community, and organizations concerned with the economic development and social services
needs of the area. The strategic planning process shall include public hearings, solicitation
of written proposals, and effective outreach efforts.
Section 2.
| Empowerment Zone Advisory Committee.
An advisory committee appointed by the Mayor in
consultation with locally elected officials shall be established to assist the City in developing
the application and the implementation strategies under the strategic plan. The Advisory
Committee shall include representatives of the Harlem Urban Development Corporation,
the Bronx Overall Economic Development Corporation, the State Department of Economic
Development, the New York State Urban Development Corporation, the business
community, and other representatives of appropriate social services and economic
development organizations.
Section 3.
Empowerment Zone Inter-Agency Working Group.
a.
The Empowerment Zone Inter-Agency Working Group
("Working Group") is hereby continued in order to act as a liaison to coordinate the
participation of City agencies in the strategic planning process.
b.
The Working Group shall be coordinated by the
Executive Director of the Economic Policy and Marketing Group under the direction of the
Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development.
