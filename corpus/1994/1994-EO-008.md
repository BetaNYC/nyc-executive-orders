EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 8
April 26, 1994
THE MAYOR’S COMMISSION TO COMBAT FAMILY VIOLENCE
(Executive Order No. 58, dated 11/29/93, Repealed)
WHEREAS, it is estimated that family violence affects as many as 279,300
households in New York City every year; and
WHEREAS, family violence is a public health crisis that respects no
boundaries of race, ethnicity, age, gender, sexual orientation, or economic status; and
WHEREAS, a priority of this administration is combatting family violence;
and
WHEREAS, the City has incorporated family violence prevention into the
New York City Police Department’s policing programs, and
WHEREAS, the development of an integrated approach to the problem of
family violence and the coordination of existing systems and services are critical to the
success of the City’s efforts in this area;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it hereby is ordered;
Section. |. Prior Order Repealed. Executive Order No. 58 of 1993, dated
November 29, 1993, hereby is repealed.
Section 2.
Establishment of a Coordinating Commission. There hereby is
established in the Mayor’s Office for Health Services a Mayor’s Family Violence
Coordinating Commission (the "Coordinating Commission"). The Director of the Mayor's
Office for Health Services and the Criminal Justice Coordinator, or their designees, shall

serve as chairpersons of the Coordinating Commission and shall coordinate and supervise
its activities.
The Coordinating Commission shall report to the Deputy Mayor for
Operations.

Section 4.
Purpose. The Coordinating Commission shall:
(a)
Formulate policies and programs relating to all aspects of services and
protocols for victims of family violence;
(b)
Develop methods to improve the coordination of systems and services
for victims of family violence;
(c)
Develop and maintain mechanisms to ensure that each affected City
agency responds appropriately to family violence situations and that there is coordination
among the agencies involved; and
(d) | Launch public education campaigns to heighten awareness of family
violence and its devastating effects on society.
Section 5.
Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately.
