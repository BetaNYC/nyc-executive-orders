EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 46

MAY 5, 2004

TRANSFER TO DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY AND
TELECOMMUNICATIONS OF CALLS RECEIVED BY SPECIFIED AGENCIES

WHEREAS, New York City 3-1-1 has successfully provided New Yorkers with rapid
and efficient access to City services; and
WHEREAS, section 1072(p) of the Charter grants the Department of Information
Technology and Telecommunications power to perform such responsibilities with respect to
information technology and telecommunications as the Mayor shall direct;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Effective June 6, 2004, or as soon as practicable thereafter, the function of
receiving calls in the Department of Health and Mental Hygiene’s call-taking unit from the
public and providing responses and appropriate referrals (including forwarding service requests)
shall be transferred to the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications,
provided that the functions of receiving calls relating to special health programs, such as those
relating to HIV/AIDS and Asthma, and calls from health care providers shall not be transferred.
§ 2.
The following functions ancillary to the function described in Section 1 shall also
be transferred to the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications:
a.
Providing administrative and computer support for functions to be transferred
within the call-taking unit; and
b.
Directly supervising the call-taking operations in relation to functions to be
transferred.
§ 3.
The Department of Citywide Administrative Services, the Department of
Information Technology and Telecommunications, and the Department of Health and Mental
Hygiene shall take all steps necessary, consistent with applicable law, to implement Sections 1
and 2 of this Order, including ensuring the transfer, pursuant to section 70(2) of the Civil Service
Law, of employees substantially engaged in the performance of the functions described in
Sections 1 and 2. Employees who are subject to pending disciplinary charges on the date of the
functional transfer, or against whom a disciplinary penalty has been assessed but not yet served
or paid on or prior to such date, shall be retained in the employment of the Department of Health

and Mental Hygiene until the resolution of the adjudicative or administrative proceedings and
until any outstanding disciplinary penalty has been served or paid.
§4.
Effective April 30, 2004, or as soon as practicable thereafter, the receipt of calls
in the Department of Finance’s Parking Violations Helpline from the public together with
providing responses and appropriate referrals (including forwarding service requests), shall be
transferred to the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications, provided
that the Department of Finance shall continue to receive calls related to a specific summons or
case.
§ 5.
In addition to performing the functions and duties specified in Executive Order
No. 31 for the year 2002 and in Sections 1, 2 and 4 of this Order, the Department of Information
Technology and Telecommunications shall continue to receive calls from the public and provide
responses and appropriate referrals with respect to the following:
a.
calls formerly handled by the Department of Education through the Chancellor’s
Customer Care Hotline;
b.
calls formerly handled by the Administration for Children’s Services through the
1-800 FOR KIDS Hotline in relation to general information concerning child care assistance;
c.
calls formerly handled by the Taxi and Limousine Commission Hotline, other
than calls by individuals or entities regulated by such commission or concerning pending
administrative matters; and
d.
calls formerly handled by the Department of Parks and Recreation.
§ 6.
The Deputy Mayor for Operations is hereby delegated the authority specified in
section 1072(p) of the Charter to direct transfers of agency telephone call-receipt and ancillary
functions and duties to the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications in
addition to those specified in this Order and in Executive Order 31 for the year 2002. Any such
transfers shall be consistent with section 70(2) of the Civil Service Law, where such provision is
applicable.
§7.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
