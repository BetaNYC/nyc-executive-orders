EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 110

COORDINATION OF CITYWIDE SERVICES FOR VETERANS

January 17, 2008

WHEREAS, veterans have played a crucial role in creating and protecting the
freedoms that all New Yorkers enjoy; and
WHEREAS, a large segment of the City’s population consists of either veterans,
or active duty or reserve members of the Armed Forces of the United States, and their
families; and
WHEREAS, a significant number of veterans are employed by the City; and
WHEREAS, the Mayor’s Office of Veterans’ Affairs (“MOVA”) was created by
Local Law No. 53 for the year 1987 to better assess the needs of the City’s veteran
population, and better coordinate service delivery to veterans and their families; and
WHEREAS, certain Mayoral agencies are responsible for providing a variety of
health and human services to address the needs of our citizens, such as public assistance,
employment and training, housing, health, medical and rehabilitation services; and
WHEREAS, the Commissioner of MOVA coordinates City services and develops
new strategies to address the needs of veterans;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:

Section 1. Agency Coordinators for Veterans’ Services. The heads of all City
agencies shall appoint a senior level staff member of appropriate managerial authority
whose duty (in addition to his or her other responsibilities) it shall be:
a. to become familiar with personnel policies affecting veterans, reservists,
and National Guardsmen, and the special needs of and services available to
such persons and family members. The appointed staff members shall make
themselves available to the Commissioner of MOVA on an as needed basis
for advice, consultation, periodic training and information-sharing; and
b. to identify all services provided by their agencies that are available to
servicemembers, veterans and their families and inform MOVA of such
services in order to establish coherent and effective service delivery
mechanisms.
§ 2. Annual Plan. The Commissioner of MOVA shall set forth an annual plan
assessing the needs of the City’s veterans community, and methods by which MOVA
shall coordinate existing, or develop new, services and partnerships to address those
needs with the appropriate City agencies, community-based organizations, and the private
sector. The annual plan shall be submitted no later than July 1 of each year.
§ 3. Veterans’ Memorial Hall. The City shall provide appropriate office space to
be utilized for veterans’ service organizations. The Commissioner shall oversee such
space which shall be known as Veterans’ Memorial Hall and ensure that the occupants
thereof utilize and maintain their offices in a manner consistent with an operational
directive to be set forth by the Commissioner of Veterans’ Affairs to be followed by all
such veterans’ service organizations.

§ 4. Executive Order No. 22, dated November 28, 1990, is hereby REPEALED.
§ 5. Effective Date. This order shall take effect immediately.
