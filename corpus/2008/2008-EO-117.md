EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 117

CENTER FOR ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITY

June 13, 2008

WHEREAS, New York City government has studied the nature of poverty in the
City and devised a blueprint to reduce it and increase access to opportunity for the poor;
and

WHEREAS, the City is pinpointing concrete ways in which it can act to ensure
the poor have the protections and resources they need to help themselves move ahead;
and

WHEREAS, it is critical that the City’s policies, programs and public/private
partnerships in the areas of education, health and human services, housing and
community development, workforce development, economic development, consumer
protection, and criminal justice are well-coordinated and embrace common principles
designed to create opportunities to advance the poor; and

WHEREAS, the City is committed to a continuous and well-coordinated interagency strategy to achieve these goals and is developing new policies, programs and
public/private partnerships; and

WHEREAS, the Center for Economic Opportunity was initially established in the
Office of the Mayor in 2006 to further develop and implement this strategy and has
already launched anti-poverty pilot programs, including Opportunity NYC which is the
nation’s first conditional cash transfer program; and

WHEREAS, the need for the continuation of the Center for Economic
Opportunity is necessary to ensure full communication, cooperation, ownership and
success in the development, implementation and evaluation of new policies, programs
and public/private partnerships;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered that:

Section 1.
The Center for Economic Opportunity (“CEO”) is continued in the
Office of the Mayor and shall be headed by an Executive Director.
§ 2.
CEO shall undertake the following activities:
a.
Poverty Research. CEO shall research poverty in terms of its
measure and impacts on certain populations as appropriate.
b.
Innovation Fund. CEO shall manage an Innovation Fund of City
resources dedicated to developing and supporting new anti-poverty
programs with City Agencies. City funds may be augmented by
other government and private resources.
c.
Developing and evaluating anti-poverty strategies. CEO shall
develop and evaluate the City’s new anti-poverty programs,
ensuring that City funds are targeted and programs are evaluated
for impact. Programs will be closely monitored and independently
evaluated. If programs do not achieve their intended results,
resources will be dedicated to more promising strategies. CEO
shall manage external consultants in evaluating all City agency
programs funded through the Innovation Fund
d.
Opportunity NYC. CEO shall be responsible for identifying
private funding for anti-poverty pilot programs, including
Opportunity NYC. CEO shall work to develop, fund and evaluate
these programs. The funds shall be deposited with the Mayor’s
Fund to Advance New York City. CEO shall have oversight of,
and responsibility for, all external contracts for design, operation
and evaluation of Opportunity NYC and will act as the designee of
the Mayor’s Fund for Opportunity NYC funds held by the Mayor’s
Fund as per the agreements of July 16, 2007 and November 16,
2007 and contracts with each private foundation.
e.
Findings and Evaluation of Results. CEO shall publish findings,
where appropriate, on program results and poverty in the City.
§ 3.
The heads of all agencies shall provide such assistance and cooperation as
deemed appropriate by the Mayor and the Executive Director.

§ 4.
This order shall take effect immediately.

Michael R. Bloomberg
Mayor
