EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 116

COORDINATOR OF FAMILY SERVICES

June 13, 2008

WHEREAS, the City’s system of providing vital health and human services to
children and families is divided among many agencies; and

WHEREAS, the City is committed to enhancing these services by investing
significant resources, including for the recruitment of additional caseworkers, as well as
strengthening training and supervision of staff overseeing children’s and family services;
and

WHEREAS, agencies tasked with identifying vulnerable children and families
have adopted comprehensive policies and procedures to investigate potential abuse and
neglect; and

WHEREAS, the collection of statistics and the dissemination of information to
the appropriate agencies is necessary for the successful delivery of services to vulnerable
children and families; and

WHEREAS, economic opportunities must be available to reduce the number of
people living in poverty, and the City has established a coordinated multi-agency policy
to create communities of economic opportunity and financial independence for
vulnerable children and families; and

WHEREAS, there is an ongoing need for improvement in technology,
communication, coordination and cooperation among the various agencies involved in
serving the needs of vulnerable children and families; and

WHEREAS, the position of Coordinator of Family Services was first established
in 2006 to facilitate the necessary inter-agency communication and cooperation to ensure
that New York City’s most vulnerable children and families are well-served and
protected;

NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered that:

§ 1.
The position of Coordinator of Family Services is hereby continued in the
Office of the Mayor. With regard to children’s and family services, the Coordinator
shall, in consultation with affected agencies:

(a)
coordinate, implement and monitor improvements in the dissemination of
information about children and family services;

(b)
request and receive reports concerning agency performance,
including appropriate use of technology, and recommend and facilitate
implementation of policies to improve agency efficiency with respect to
children and family services. The content of such reports and any such
policies for reform shall be established by the Coordinator;

(c)
expand agency partnerships with Community Based Organizations
that advocate for child safety;

(d)
aid in the development of medical standards utilized by New York
City hospitals to diagnose child abuse and neglect;

(e)
develop, coordinate and implement programs and services for
youth in the foster care system, youth aging out of the foster care system
and youth in the juvenile detention system;

(f)
foster the increase, utilization and expansion of Child Advocacy
Centers throughout the City;

(g)
design and implement a Citywide public awareness campaign and
website to prevent and stop child abuse and child neglect;

(h)
 coordinate agency efforts to improve access to early intervention
public health insurance for children in City-funded programs, subsidized
child care and head start;

(i)
develop, coordinate and implement plans to increase access to
quality mental health services for children in foster care and the juvenile
justice system;

(j)
develop, with the Department of Juvenile Justice, prevention,
aftercare, and alternatives to detention programs;

(k)
develop an overall accountability model for social services
agencies providing services to children, families and individuals;

(l)
implement a family services website to provide information to
agency clients and personnel on programs, policies and procedures, as

2

well as to conduct eligibility screening;

(m)
implement an enhanced 311 system for City residents seeking
health and human services;

(n)
develop partnerships with other branches and levels of
government, including with the Office of the Speaker of the City Council
to increase food stamp utilization, better school meal participation and
access to healthy foods in high needs communities;

(o)
implement a “one-stop” service center for benefits and case
assistance;

(p)
facilitate a case coordination partnership with the Criminal Justice
Coordinator and the Family Court case coordinator; and

(q)
establish a network of agency liaisons, to be designated
individually by each human services agency, to facilitate service
coordination.

§ 2.
Affected agencies shall provide such assistance and cooperation as
deemed appropriate by the Mayor and the Coordinator.

§ 3.
For purposes of Sections 1 and 2 of this Order, the term “affected
agencies” shall include, but not be limited to, the following agencies: Department of
Health and Mental Hygiene, Human Resources Administration, Administration for
Children’s Services, Department of Youth and Community Development, Department of
Homeless Services, Department for the Aging, Health and Hospitals Corporation,
Department of Correction, Department of Probation, Department of Juvenile Justice,
Department of Education, New York City Housing Authority, Mayor’s Office to Combat
Domestic Violence, Mayor’s Office of Adult Education, Office of Citywide Health
Insurance Access and the HIV Health and Human Services Planning Council.

§ 4. This Order shall take effect immediately.
