EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 429
October 15, 2013
COORDINATION AND IMPLEMENTATION OF MATTERS PERTAINING TO
STORMWATER CONTROLS AND MUNICIPAL SEPARATE STORM SEWER SYSTEM
PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
WHEREAS, every year, approximately 45 inches of rain fall in New York City,
generating an average of 165 billion gallons of stormwater, and approximately half of that
rainfall makes its way into the City’s combined sewer system, with much of the balance flowing
directly into surrounding waterways through the City’s separate storm sewers;
WHEREAS, a number of New York City agencies plan, fund, and implement measures
to control stormwater runoff to prevent pollution of surface waters, and
WHEREAS, the Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) operates and maintains
both the combined sewer system and the majority of the Municipal Separate Storm Sewer
System (MS4);
WHEREAS, the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation
(NYSDEC), pursuant to authority delegated by the United States Environmental Protection
Agency, regulates discharges of pollutants to surface waters, including discharges from the
City’s combined sewer system and from the City’s MS4;
WHEREAS, NYSDEC has issued to DEP fourteen (14) State Pollutant Discharge
Elimination System (SPDES) permits authorizing discharges from the City’s fourteen municipal
wastewater treatment plants and from combined and separate sewers in the drainage basins of
each of those wastewater treatment plants;
WHEREAS, the City, acting by and through DEP, is currently negotiating a new SPDES
permit for the City’s MS4 with NYSDEC that will supersede the provisions of the fourteen
wastewater treatment plant SPDES permits addressing discharges from the City’s MS4, and will
include requirements for stormwater controls to reduce pollution from the City’s MS4, including
“good housekeeping” provisions requiring the City to minimize exposure of stormwater to
potential sources of contamination from municipal operations; and
WHEREAS, because a number of New York City agencies in addition to DEP have an
important role in planning, funding, and implementing measures to control pollution of surface
waters, both through the combined sewer system and through the City’s MS4, the City has

established a Green Infrastructure Task Force to oversee and coordinate the management of
stormwater in combined-sewer drainage areas, and a Stormwater Controls Working Group to
oversee and coordinate the management of stormwater in areas serviced by the City’s MS4;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Delegation.
The Commissioner of the Department of Environmental
Protection is hereby directed to act on behalf of the City, and to coordinate the efforts of City
agencies, with respect to all matters relating tothe City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit
Requirements, in connection with the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System
(NPDES) program, administered by NYSDEC through the SPDES program, and may enter into
agreements with NYSDEC or any other State or federal agency on behalf of the City with respect
to such matters. For purposes of this Order, the term “the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit
Requirements” includes the stormwater provisions of the fourteen (14) wastewater treatment
plant SPDES permits including, but not limited to, Combined Sewer Overflow Best Management
Practices, as well as the provisions of the MS4 SPDES permit.
§ 2. Cooperation in Planning and Implementing Stormwater Controls. Heads and staff of
all mayoral agencies with any role in planning, designing, funding, implementing, operating,
and/or maintaining stormwater controls affecting the City’s combined sewer system and/or the
City’s MS4, together with the Department of Education (DOE) upon the written concurrence of
the Chancellor or his or her designee, shall cooperate with DEP by:
(i) providing to DEP all information in their possession reasonably necessary for
negotiating and/or complying with the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit Requirements,
(ii) working with the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) to identify funding,
within available appropriations, and implementing, in coordination with DEP, any
stormwater control measures included in the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit
Requirements that fall within their agencies’ responsibilities, jurisdiction, or authority;
(iii) creating and maintaining any necessary records and preparing any reports or other
documentation required in connection with their agencies’ implementation of measures
included in the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit Requirements that fall within their
agencies’ responsibilities; and
(iv) providing assistance and support to DEP within their areas of expertise, including but
not limited to provision of relevant trainings and education to staff and external parties,
as needed to facilitate efficient compliance with the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit
Requirements.
§ 3. Funding of Costs. In the event of any additional or unforeseen costs to the City
(including but not limited to costs related to remedial measures or resulting from enforcement
actions) in relation to compliance with the City’s Stormwater SPDES Permit Requirements that
fall within the responsibilities, jurisdiction or authority of any mayoral agency, the affected
agency and OMB shall cooperate to identify an appropriate source of funds to pay for such costs.

§ 4. Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately, provided that in the case
of DOE, this Order shall take effect upon the written concurrence of the Chancellor of DOE or
his or her designee.
