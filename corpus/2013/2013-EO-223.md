EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 223
EMERGENCY NUISANCE ABATEMENT THROUGH DEBRIS REMOVAL
January 22, 2013
WHEREAS, Executive Order 205, issued December 27, 2012, contains a proclamation
extending a state of emergency in the City of New York, and such proclamation remains in effect
for a period not to exceed thirty days or until rescinded, whichever occurs first;
WHEREAS, the President of the United States signed a Major Disaster Declaration under
the Stafford Act on October 30, 2012;
WHEREAS, the Governor of the State of New York has issued Executive Order No. 63,
dated Nov. 5, 2012, entitled Declaring Certain Conditions Caused by Hurricane Sandy in and
Around Federally Declared Counties to be Public Nuisances Impacting Public Health and
Ordering the Removal of Those Conditions, which included determination that debris poses a
persistent threat to the security, life and health of persons in those areas;
WHEREAS, the recent storm caused trees to fall and resulted in large amounts of debris
in numerous parts of the City, causing conditions that are a danger to health and safety; and
WHEREAS, the City has established an inter-agency Hurricane Sandy Debris Removal
Task Force, which has been working on the removal of downed trees and debris;
NOW THEREFORE, it is hereby ordered:
§ 1. The Department of Sanitation, the Task Force, and other relevant City agencies shall

work with relevant state and federal agencies and departments to clear debris and downed trees
in affected areas, on both public and private property.
§ 2. City agencies shall enter private residential property as needed to remove debris in
accordance with the Governor’s order.
Where necessary, such agencies shall utilize available
procedures under state and local law to gain access.
§ 3.
The Commissioner of the Business Integrity Commission is authorized to waive
license and registration requirements for the collection, removal or disposal of trade waste where
the Commissioner determines it is appropriate to do so to effectuate the purposes of this Order.
§ 4. This order shall take effect immediately.
