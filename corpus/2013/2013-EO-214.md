EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 214
January 7, 2013
TRANSFER OF SPECIFIED FUNCTIONS AND RELATED STAFF
OF THE NEW YORK CITY HEALTH AND HOSPITALS CORPORATION TO THE NEW YORK
CITY DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND MENTAL HYGIENE
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. Effective February 1, 2013, or as soon as practicable thereafter as a transfer may be
effectuated pursuant to the terms and conditions of a duly executed memorandum of understanding
between the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (‘DOHMH”) and the New
York City Health and Hospitals Corporation (“HHC), all HHC staff who are substantially engaged in
assisting DOHMH by performing the following functions shall be transferred to DOHMH:
oversight, monitoring, and administration of DOHMH’s Central Medical Supply pharmacy
operations, DOHMH’s Information Technology Initiatives Bureau, and DOFIMH’s Office of Grants
Administration and Claiming; administrative direction of correctional health; and functions ancillary
thereto.
§ 2.
The Commissioner of Citywide Administrative Services and the Commissioner of
DOHMH shall take all steps necessary, consistent with applicable law, to implement this Order,
including ensuring the transfer of necessary employees substantially engaged in the performance of
the functions to be assumed pursuant to this Order. Employees who are subject to pending
disciplinary charges on the date of the functional transfer, or against whom a disciplinary penalty has
been assessed but not yet served or paid on or prior to such date, shall be retained in the employment
of HHC until the resolution of the adjudicative or administrative proceedings and until any
outstanding disciplinary penalty has been served or paid.
§ 3.
With the purpose of ensuring continuity in operation of the functions described in
Section 1, DOHMH and HHC may make appropriate arrangements, prior to the date of the functional
transfer, with respect to appropriate disposition of contracts and agreements entered into by HI-IC in
relation to the functions being transferred and other appropriate matters, including but not limited to
budgetary arrangements.
§ 4.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
