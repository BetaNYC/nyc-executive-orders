EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 85
February 6, 2006
DEPUTY MAYORS
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. There shall be seven Deputy Mayors. One shall be designated the First Deputy
Mayor, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Education and Community Development,
one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Legal Affairs, one shall be designated the Deputy
Mayor for Economic Development and Rebuilding, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor
for Administration, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Government Relations and one
shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Health and Human Services.
§ 2. The First Deputy Mayor shall:
a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Cultural Affairs, the
Department of Parks and Recreation, the Department of Design and Construction, the
Department of Consumer Affairs, the Commission for the United Nations, Consular Corps and
Protocol, the Commission on Women’s Issues and all administrations, departments, agencies,
boards, offices, committees and commissions except as otherwise provided herein, provided that
the Law Department, the Police Department, the Fire Department, the Office of Emergency
Management, the Department of Investigation, the Office of Management and Budget and the
Office of Labor Relations shall report directly to the Mayor.

b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Mayor’s Offices of Correspondence,
Fiscal and Administrative Management, Appointments, Scheduling and Advance, and Special
Projects and Community Events and supervise City sponsored public events of the City.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Art Commission, the Landmarks
Preservation Commission, the Mayor’s Fund to Advance New York City, the Gracie Mansion
Conservancy, the Central Park Conservancy, libraries, museums, performing arts organizations,
gardens and zoos.
d. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 3. The First Deputy Mayor hereby is delegated the authority to perform all functions,
powers or duties of the Mayor which the Mayor is authorized to delegate in the event that the
Mayor is unable to perform such functions, powers or duties for a reason specified in subdivision
a of section 10 of the New York City Charter.
§ 4. The Deputy Mayor for Education and Community Development shall:
a. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning, programs and initiatives.
b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Youth and Community
Development and the Mayor’s Volunteer Center.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Department of Education, the
New York City School Construction Authority, the New York City Educational Construction
Fund, the City University of New York, the City University Construction Fund, the New York
City Housing Authority and the Mayor's Commission on Youth Empowerment Services.

d. Work with the other Deputy Mayors in coordination with the Office of Management
and Budget and the Office of Operations, to ensure the implementation of Mayoral policies and

e. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
f. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 5. The Deputy Mayor for Legal Affairs shall:
a. Provide counsel to the Mayor on legal matters.
b. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Coordinator of Administrative Justice,
the Commission on Human Rights, the Office to Combat Domestic Violence, the Office for
People with Disabilities, the Office of Immigrant Affairs and the Office of Veterans Affairs.
c. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Mayor's Committee on the
Judiciary, the Conflicts of Interest Board, the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings, the
Voter Assistance Commission, the Civilian Complaint Review Board, the Commission to
Combat Police Corruption and the Equal Employment Practices Commission.
d. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 6. The Deputy Mayor for Economic Development and Rebuilding shall:
a. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning, and programs designed to
foster the economic development or growth of the City, including those relating to the City's
overall business climate, the attraction, retention and expansion of domestic and global business
enterprises in the City, efforts to secure unique events, commercial, industrial and residential

development, waterfront development, energy, foreign trade and investment, government
regulation, film, theater and broadcasting, entertainment and sports facility development,
infrastructure, tourism and air, land and sea freight.
b. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding the City's physical and economic response to
the destruction of the World Trade Center, including plans for the redevelopment of the financial
district and Lower Manhattan.
c. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of City Planning and the
Office of Film, Theater, and Broadcasting.
d. Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the City Planning Commission, the
New York City Housing Development Corporation, the United Nations Development Corp., the
New York City Sports Commission and New York City Sports Development Corporation, the
Board of Standards and Appeals, the Taxi and Limousine Commission, the New York City
Marketing Development Corporation, the Rent Guidelines Board, the Tax Commission, the Tax
Appeals Tribunal and the New York City Loft Board.
e. Serve as Chairperson of the Commercial Incentive Boundary Area Commission and
the Industrial and Commercial Incentive Board.
f. Supervise and coordinate the activities of the Department of Small Business Services,
the Department of Housing Preservation and Development, the Department of Buildings, the
Department of Transportation, the Department of Environmental Protection, the Department of
Finance, the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications, the Office of
Operations and the Office of Environmental Coordination, and the Economic Development
Corporation as provided in the Amended and Restated Contracts between the City and the

purpose of issuing bonds for, or managing, any project relating to any matter described in this

§ 7. The Deputy Mayor for Administration shall:
a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Citywide Administrative
Services, the Department of Sanitation, the Criminal Justice Coordinator, the Business Integrity
Commission, the Office of Midtown Enforcement, the Mayor’s Office of Contract Services, the
Mayor’s Office of Communications and the Mayor’s Press Office.
b. Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning and programs related to
the management of the agencies that report directly to the Mayor, except for the Department of
Investigation.
c. Develop, supervise and coordinate policies and programs relating to personnel and
management practices.
d. Act for and on behalf of the Mayor in the exercise of all functions, powers and duties
which the Mayor may have pursuant to the New York State Local Finance Law, as the same may
from time to time be amended, the New York State Financial Emergency Act for the City of
New York, as the same may from time to time be amended, Article 10 of the Public Authorities
Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, the New York City Municipal Water
Finance Authority Act, as the same may from time to time be amended, and the New York City
Transitional Authority Act, as the same may from time to time be amended, including, but not

e. Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the City, official statements of the
City relating to the issuance of the City's obligations.
f. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to the execution or
authorization of contracts, franchises, concessions, revocable consents, and other agreements.
g. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor, including authorizations and

i. Maintain liaison with the Police Pension Fund, Fire Department Pension Fund, New
York City Employees’ Retirement System, Teachers’ Retirement System and the Board of
Education Retirement System.
j. Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in negotiating, executing and
delivering any and all agreements, instruments and other documents necessary or desirable to
effectuate any of the matters referred to in this section or any of the programs, projects and
activities of any of the entities referred to in this section.

§ 8. The Deputy Mayor for Government Relations shall:
a. Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Office of Intergovernmental Affairs, the
Office of City Legislative Affairs, the Office of State Legislative Affairs, the Office of Federal
Affairs and the Department of Records and Information Services.
b. Serve as the Mayor's representative, either directly or through the Offices of City and
State Legislative Affairs, on, and maintain liaison with, regional bodies and intergovernmental
agencies operating within the New York metropolitan region.
c. Maintain liaison, either directly or through the Office of Federal Affairs with the
United States Conference of Mayors, the National League of Cities, the New York Conference of
Mayors and other similar bodies.
d. Develop and implement the Mayor’s city, state and federal legislative agenda, serve as
the Mayor’s primary liaison to city, state and federal legislators and staff, supervise and
coordinate the legislative agenda of Mayoral agencies and develop, supervise and coordinate
policies and programs to secure funds from the federal and state governments.
e. Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
f. Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
