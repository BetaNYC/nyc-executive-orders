EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 10
June 24, 2015
COMMISSION ON GENDER EQUITY
WHEREAS, it is the policy of the City of New York to ensure that the women of the City
live with dignity and equity, and be free from violence and discrimination based upon gender;
and
WHEREAS, it is further the policy of the City of New York that women be assured of the
benefits of equal opportunity and the full utilization of their talents, no matter their race,
ethnicity, class or sexual orientation; and
WHEREAS, it is further the policy of the City of New York generally to promote the
economic, social and physical well-being of women, and to develop and support policies that
promote opportunities for women in all areas, including, but not limited to, employment,
housing, child care, education and health; and
WHEREAS, it is necessary and desirable that the City of New York establish a program
for the enactment and implementation of these policies;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered:
Section 1. Establishment of the Commission on Gender Equity. There is hereby
established under the direction of the Office of the Mayor a Commission on Gender Equity
(hereinafter the "Commission").

(a) The Commission shall consist of a Chair and no fewer than thirty members, and one
or more Honorary Members, all of whom shall be appointed by the Mayor and shall serve
at the pleasure of the Mayor.
(b) The Chair of the New York City Commission on Human Rights shall serve as a nonvoting ex officio member. Members other than the Chair, the ex officio member and any
Honorary Member shall be appointed for terms of three years. Members shall serve
without compensation.
(c) The Mayor shall appoint an Executive Director of the Commission to assist the
Commission in carrying out its duties, and any other such staff as the Mayor authorizes.
§ 2. Duties of the Commission. The Commission shall:
(a) Serve as an advisory group to the Mayor on initiatives and methods to achieve the
goals of the Mayor’s platform to reduce inequality, with a focus on gender-based
inequality;
(b) Advocate for women and the support programs that have been created to remove
barriers to full participation in all areas of women's personal and work lives;
(c) Study the nature and extent of both the intentional and unintentional discrimination
that women face in the City of New York, and their impacts on the economic, civic
and social well-being of women;
(d) Make recommendations to the Mayor regarding legislative or executive action to
improve the lives of women;
(e) Educate the public about women's issues; and
(f) Support and work collaboratively with the network of organizations in the public and
private sectors working to expand opportunities for women.
§ 3. Powers of the Commission.
(a) The Commission is authorized to work in conjunction with federal and state
government agencies, where appropriate, and all agencies of the City of New York shall
cooperate with the Commission in the performance of its duties.

(b) The Chair may establish such committees of the Commission as may be deemed
necessary for the performance and carrying out of its duties and functions.
§ 4. Prior Order Revoked. Executive Order No. 29, dated October 25, 2002, is hereby revoked.
§ 5. Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately.
