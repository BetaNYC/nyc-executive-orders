THe City oF New York
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 13
December 15, 2015
M/WBE ADVISORY COMMITTEE
WHEREAS, it is the policy of the City of New York to promote economic growth in a
manner that ensures that city residents and businesses from all communities and backgrounds
can participate in and contribute to the economy of the city.
WHEREAS, Minority and Women-owned Business Enterprises (“M/WBEs”) are
amongst the fastest growing small businesses that create jobs and invest in local communities.
WHEREAS, notwithstanding that M/WBEs are strong drivers of economic growth, they
have not historically been awarded contracts at a level commensurate with their representation in
the marketplace.
WHEREAS, to advance the City’s policy of inclusivity and in recognition of the fact that
M/WBE firms remain persistently underrepresented among City contractors, the City of New
York has made a commitment to awarding more than $16 billion in awards to M/WBE over the
next ten years.
WHEREAS, the City
of New York benefits from input from, outreach to and cooperation
with M/WBE firms and advocates in order to ensure greater participation by all in City work.
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered:
§1. Establishment of the M/WBE Advisory Committee. There is hereby established in the
Office of the Mayor an M/WBE Advisory Committee (hereinafter the “Committee”).
(a) The Committee shall consist of a Chair and no fewer than ten members, all of whom
shall be appointed by the Mayor and shall serve at the pleasure of the Mayor.

(b) The Citywide M/WBE Director shall serve as an ex officio member and shall direct
the operations of the Committee in order to facilitate the Committee’s ability to
provide advice to the Mayor.
(©) Members other than the Chair and any ex officio member shall be appointed for terms
of three years. Members shall serve without compensation.
§ 2. Duties of the Committee. The Committee shall:
(a) Serve as an advisory group to the Mayor on M/WBE issues and on methods to
increase M/WBE program participation.
(b) Provide information to the M/WBE community about programs and opportunities for
M/WBES in order to increase participation by M/WBES in City contracting, including
by encouraging M/WBEs to certify with the City.
(c) Educate relevant stakeholders and constituencies in order to support efforts by the
City to increase M/WBE opportunities through regulatory and legislative changes.
§ 3. Powers of the Committee,
(a) The Chair is authorized to direct the Committee to gather or disseminate information
and convene meetings in order to improve information sharing about M/WBE issues
and opportunities and to develop and provide advice.
§ 4. Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately.
Bill de Blasio
Mayor
