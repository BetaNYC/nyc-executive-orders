THe City oF New York
OFFICE OF THE Mayor
New YORK, N.Y. 10007
Executive Order No.
64
May 24,
1982
The Doris C. Freedman
Award
ue
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of
New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Award Established.
The Doris C. Freedman
Award (the "Award") is hereby established.
This Award shall
be presented annually to an individual or organization
selected by the Mayor in recognition for "a contribution
to the people of the City of New York that greatly enriches
the public environment."
Section 2. Purpose.
This Award is dedicated to the
memory of Doris C. Freedman who labored tirelessly to
enliven and humanize the cultural environment of our city.
Doris C. Freedman, the first New York City Director of
Cultural Affairs, President of City Walls, The Municipal
Art Society and The Public Art Fund, was active in numerous
community and educational institutions.
The purpose of the Award is to recognize outstanding
efforts to improve the public environment and encourage
greater interest and appreciation for works of art displayed
in public places.
Section 3.
Award Recipient.
The recipient of this
Award will be an artist, architect, civic activist, corporation, neighborhood group, or other organization or individual whose actions have had a substantial impact on the
visual landscape of New York City.
It is intended that the
Award be given in recognition of a continous effort on the
part of the recipient to achieve this end.
Section 4.
Selection Process.
Annually, the Mayor's
office will solicit nominations from those organizations
#CY

active in the preservation and enhancement of a livable
city, such as, The Municipal Art Society, The Landmarks
Conservancy, The Public Art Fund, The Parks Council,
The Architectural League, The New York Chapter of the
American Institute of Architects, The Fine Arts Federation,
The New York Commission on Cultural Affairs and the New
York City Art Commission, and other interested groups.
The Mayor will select the final recipient, in consultation with the Chairman of the Landmarks Preservation
Commission, the Commissioner of Cultural Affairs and the
Commissioner of Parks and Recreation.
Section 5.
Award Presentation Date.
The 1982 Award
shall be presented within six months of the effective date
of this order.
Thereafter, the Award shall be presented
on April 25 each year in commemoration of Doris C. Freedman's
birthday.
Section 6.
Effective Date.
This Executive Order shall
take effect immediately.
Edward TI. ‘Koch
MAYOR
