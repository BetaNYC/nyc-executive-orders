EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 2

JANUARY 1, 2002

DEPUTY MAYORS

By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is hereby
ordered:
Section 1. There shall be five Deputy Mayors. One shall be designated the Deputy
Mayor for Operations, one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Policy, one shall be
designated the Deputy Mayor for Administration, one shall be designated the Deputy
Mayor for Legal Affairs and one shall be designated the Deputy Mayor for Economic
Development and Recovery.
§ 2.
The Deputy Mayor for Operations shall:
a.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of all administrations,
departments, agencies, boards, offices, committees, and commissions
except as otherwise provided herein, provided that, except with respect to
matters pertaining to their departmental budgets, the Law Department, the
Police Department, the Fire Department, the Department of Investigation,
the Office of Management and Budget, the Criminal Justice Coordinator,
and the Office of Communications shall report directly to the Mayor.
b.
Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning and programs
designed to implement and enhance the fiscal operations and financial and
business management practices of the City, including those related to
labor relations.
c.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Mayor's Office of
Operations, the Office of City Legislative Affairs, the Office of State
Legislative Affairs, the Mayor's Office in Washington, D.C., the Office of
Labor Relations and the Office of Environmental Coordination.

d.
Serve as the Mayor's representative, either directly or through the Offices
of City and State Legislative Affairs, on, and maintain liaison with,
regional bodies and intergovernmental agencies operating within the New
York metropolitan region.
e.
Maintain liaison, either directly or through the Mayor's Office in
Washington, D.C., with the United States Conference of Mayors, the
National League of Cities, the New York Conference of Mayors and other
similar bodies.
f.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Metropolitan
Transportation Authority and the New York State Public Service
Commission.
g.
Supervise and coordinate the preparation of the City's Revenue, Expense
and Capital Budgets.
h.
Develop, supervise and coordinate policies and programs relating to
personnel and management practices.
i.
Succeed to the powers and duties of the "Deputy Mayor – City
Administrator of the City" under section 7384 of the Unconsolidated Laws
(Chapter 1016 of the Laws of 1969).
j.
Act for and on behalf of the Mayor in the exercise of all functions, powers
and duties which the Mayor may have pursuant to the New York State
Local Finance Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, the
New York State Financial Emergency Act for the City of New York, as
the same may from time to time be amended, Article 10 of the Public
Authorities Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, the New
York City Municipal Water Finance Authority Act, as the same may from
time to time be amended, and the New York City Transitional Authority
Act, as the same may from time to time be amended, including, but not
limited to, full power to execute for and on behalf of the Mayor, any
agreement, amendment to such agreement or other document pursuant to
such laws.

k.
Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the Mayor, a certificate
approving any borrowing of funds or pledge of assets or revenues by the
New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation which would require the
approval of the Mayor pursuant to the "Memorandum of Agreement
Between New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation and The City
of New York Relating to Operating Expenditures by the Corporation”,
dated July 1, 1970.
l.
Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the City, official
statements of the City relating to the issuance of the City's obligations.
m.
Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board,
committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
n.
Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to the
execution or authorization of contracts, franchises, revocable consents,
and other agreements.
o.
Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor, including
authorizations and approvals to be made or given by the Mayor pursuant
to sections 384, 824 and 1802 of the Charter, in relation to the
authorization or execution of deeds and satisfactions, leases, easements,
mortgages, and any other grants or estates or interests in real property and
licenses, permits or other agreements affecting the use of real property.
p.
Perform any function, power or duty which the Mayor has and is
authorized to delegate pursuant to New York City Charter sections 217,
219, 329 and 1301(2)(b), and pursuant to Administrative Code section 5-
304.
q.
Approve and sign applications to secure grant funds, submitted pursuant to
provisions of the New York State Environmental Conservation Law or the
rules or regulations of the State Department of Environmental
Conservation, and to sign any other documents as may be necessary to
secure such funds.
r.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.

§ 3.
The Deputy Mayor for Operations hereby is delegated the authority to
perform all functions, powers or duties of the Mayor which the Mayor is
authorized to delegate in the event that the Mayor is unable to perform such
functions, powers or duties for a reason specified in subdivision a of section 10 of
the New York City Charter.
§ 4.
The Deputy Mayor for Policy shall:
a.
Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning, programs and
initiatives.
b.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Mayor's Voluntary Action
Center, the Administration for Children's Services and the Department of
Youth and Community Development.
c.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Board of Education,
the New York City School Construction Authority, the City University of
New York, the New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation and the
Mayor's Commission on Youth Empowerment Services.
d.
Succeed to the powers and duties of the "Health Services Administrator"
under section 7384 of the Unconsolidated Laws (Chapter 1016 of the
Laws of 1969) and shall act on behalf of the City of New York, in
accordance with subdivision 1 of section 8 of the Facilities Development
Act (Chapter 359 of the Laws of 1968), for the purpose of executing
amendments to sublease agreements heretofore made by and among the
New York State Housing Finance Agency, the Health and Mental Hygiene
Facilities Improvements Corporation and the City of New York, acting
through the Health Services Administration.
e.
Work with the Deputy Mayor for Operations in coordination with the
Office of Management and Budget and the Office of Operations, to ensure
the implementation of Mayoral policies and initiatives.
f.
Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board,
committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
g.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 5.
The Deputy Mayor for Administration shall:

a.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Cultural
Affairs, the Department of Parks and Recreation and the Commission for
the United Nations, Consular Corps and Protocol.
b.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of City Hall and supervise City
sponsored public events of the City.
c.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Art Commission, the
Landmarks Preservation Commission, the Central Park Conservancy,
libraries, museums, performing arts organizations, gardens and zoos.
d.
Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board,
committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 6.
The Deputy Mayor for Legal Affairs shall:
a.
Provide counsel to the Mayor on legal matters.
b.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Office for People with
Disabilities, the Office of Immigrant Affairs, the Office to Combat
Domestic Violence, the Commission on the Status of Women and the New
York City Commission on Human Rights.
c.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Mayor's Committee
on the Judiciary, the Conflicts of Interest Board, the Office of
Administrative Trials and Hearings, the Voter Assistance Commission and
the Equal Employment Practices Commission.
d.
Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board,
committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
e.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 7.
The Deputy Mayor for Economic Development and Recovery shall:
a.
Advise and assist the Mayor regarding all policies, planning, and programs
designed to foster the economic growth of the City, including those
relating to the City's overall business climate, the attraction, retention and
expansion of domestic and global business enterprises in the City, efforts
to secure the 2012 Olympic Games, commercial and industrial
development, energy, foreign trade and investment, government

regulation, film, theater and broadcasting, entertainment and sports facility
development, and air, land and sea freight.
b.
Advise and assist the Mayor regarding the City's physical and economic
response to the destruction of the World Trade Center, including plans for
the redevelopment of the financial district and Lower Manhattan.
c.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of City
Planning and the Office of Film, Theater, and Broadcasting.
d.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the City Planning
Commission, the New York City Housing Development Corporation, the
United Nations Development Corp., the New York City Sports
Commission and New York City Sports Development Corporation, the
Board of Standards and Appeals, and the New York City Loft Board.
e.
Serve as Chairperson of the Commercial Incentive Boundary Area
Commission and the Industrial and Commercial Incentive Board.
f.
Direct and supervise the activities of the Department of Business Services,
the Department of Consumer Affairs, the Economic Development
Corporation as provided in the Amended and Restated Contract between
the City and the Economic Development Corporation, and take such
actions thereunder as are reserved to the Deputy Mayor.
g.
Direct and coordinate the activities of all administrations, departments,
agencies, boards, offices, committees and commissions with respect to
energy policies, planning and programs.
h.
Maintain liaison with the New York State Department of Economic
Development, the City Empowerment Zone, the Empire State
Development Corporation, the Port Authority of New York and New
Jersey, the Power Authority of the State of New York, the New York
Convention and Visitors Bureau, the Lower Manhattan Development
Corporation and other governmental agencies and offices with respect to
matters of City economic development, energy policies and programs.
i.
Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor, including
authorizations and approvals to be made or given by the Mayor pursuant

to sections 384, 824 and 1802 of the Charter, in relation to the
authorization or execution of deeds and satisfactions, leases, easements,
mortgages and any other grants of estates or interests in real property and
licenses, permits or other agreements affecting the use of real property by
or involving the Economic Development Corporation, including any such
authorization or approval in relation to real property under the jurisdiction
of the Department of Citywide Administrative Services made for the
purpose of a project of the Economic Development Corporation.
j.
Advise and assist the Mayor regarding tax policies.
k.
Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to any
contracts with or involving the New York City Industrial Development
Agency.
1.
Act for and on behalf of the Mayor in the exercise of all functions, powers
and duties which the Mayor may have pursuant to the New York State
Local Finance Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, the
New York State Financial Emergency Act for the City of New York, as
the same may from time to time be amended, Article 10 of the Public
Authorities Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, and the
New York City Municipal Water Finance Authority Act, as the same may
from time to time be amended, and the New York City Transitional
Authority Act, as the same may from time to time be amended, including,
but not limited to, full power to execute for and on behalf of the Mayor,
any agreement, amendment to such agreement or other document pursuant
to such laws.
m.
Perform any function, power or duty of the Mayor in relation to the
execution or authorization of such franchises and revocable consents as
relate to the duties of the Deputy Mayor for Economic Development.
n.
Perform any function, power or duty which the Mayor has and is
authorized to delegate pursuant to New York City Charter section
1301(2)(b) and Administrative Code section 5-304.

o.
Approve and sign applications to secure grant funds, submitted pursuant to
provisions of the New York State Environmental Conservation Law or the
rules and regulations of the State Department of Environmental
Conservation, and to sign any other documents as may be necessary to
secure such funds.
p.
Represent the Mayor, when so directed by the Mayor, on any board,
committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
q.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§ 8. Executive Order No. 20, dated March 23, 1995, and Executive Order No. 31,
dated August 29, 1996, as amended by Executive Order No. 46, dated
May 27, 1999, are hereby REPEALED.
§ 9.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
