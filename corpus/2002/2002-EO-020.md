EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 20

JULY 7, 2002

PROCLAMATION OF A STATE OF EMERGENCY

Whereas, Employees of Jamaica Buses, Queens Surface Corporation and Triboro
Coach Corporation, all of which companies have franchise contracts with the New York
City Department of Transportation to provide service in areas of the City where bus
service is not provided by the New York City Transit Authority, commenced a strike on
June 17, 2002, and such strike has caused serious disruption of the City's transportation
system; and
Whereas, It is necessary to insure public safety and the orderly transportation of
people and goods in the City of New York; and
Whereas, The strike poses an immediate risk of an increase in the response time
of emergency vehicles which would endanger the lives and health of the City's residents,
commuters and visitors, inability to gain access to routine medical care, the loss of wages
for thousands of persons, the loss of revenue, on a daily basis, for businesses in the City,
and lost revenues for the City and the Board of Education, and significant increased
expenditures by the City of New York;
Whereas, These circumstances constitute an imminent peril to the public health
and safety requiring the adoption of special rules, regulations, procedures and restrictions
as emergency measures; and
Whereas, Executive Law §24 authorizes the Mayor to promulgate local
emergency orders upon a finding that the public safety is imperiled by the event of a
public emergency or in the event of reasonable apprehension of immediate danger
thereof, to protect life and property and to bring an emergency situation under control;
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it
is hereby ordered:

Section 1. State of Emergency. A state of emergency is hereby declared to exist
within the City of New York.

§ 2. Commuter van and for-hire vehicle provisions. The following provisions
shall apply to vehicles licensed by the New York City Taxi and Limousine Commission:

(a) All for-hire vehicles and commuter vans licensed as such by the Taxi and
Limousine Commission are authorized, for the duration of the emergency, to pick
up passengers without prearrangement at such locations within the City as may be
designated by the Chairperson of the Taxi and Limousine Commission.
(b) All commuter vans licensed as such by the Taxi and Limousine Commission are
authorized to operate outside of the geographical area set forth in their operation
authorization, subject to geographic limits as may be designated by the
Chairperson of the Taxi and Limousine Commission, and are further authorized to
accept and discharge passengers on streets within such geographic limits which
otherwise constitute bus routes and at points which otherwise constitute bus stops
as approved by the Chairperson.

§ 3. Effective date. This executive order shall take effect immediately.
