THE City oF New Yorn
OFFICE OF THE MAYoR
New York, N.Y. 10007
Executive Order No.
111
March 20, 1987
ESTABLISHMENT OF AN AUDIT COMMITTEE
FOR THE CITY OF NEW YORK
By virtue of the power vested in me as Mayor of the
City of New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
Prior Order Revoked.
Executive Order
No.
29 of February 6, 1979 is hereby revoked.
Section 2.
Audit Committee Continued.
The Audit
Committee of the City of New York (the "Committee") is
hereby continued and shall consist of the Mayor, the
Comptroller, the President of the City Council, each of whom
shall serve ex officio (the "City Members") and four other
persons (the "Private Members") as provided in Section 3.
The members of the Committee shall elect a Private Member as
Chairperson of the Committee for an annual term commencing
on March 1 of each year.
Section 3.
Private Members.
The Mayor shall appoint
as Private Members of the Committee four individuals, two of
whom shall be appointed upon the recommendation of the
Comptroller.
The Private Members shall include (i) two
individuals with expertise in municipal finance, and (ii)
two officers or employees of two different firms of
independent public accountants which are not engaged by the
City of New York.
Two Private Members, one of whom shall
have been recommended by the Comptroller, shall serve for
two-year terms commencing March 1, 1987; and the remaining
two Private Members shall serve for one-year terms
commencing March 1, 1987.
Succeeding Private Members shall
serve for two-year terms.
Private Members shall continue in
office until their successors have been appointed and
qualified.
Private Members shall serve without salary,
except that each Private Member shall be entitled to

reimbursement for his actual and necessary expenses incurred
in the performance of his official duties as a Private
Member of the Committee and shall also receive a per diem
allowance of one hundred fifty dollars ($150) when rendering
services as a Private Member, provided that the aggregate
per diem allowance to any one Private Member in any one
annual term of office shall not exceed the sum of one
thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500).
Section 4.
Functions of the Audit Committee.
The
Committee shall assist in the determination of areas of
inquiry for, review the progress of, and evaluate the
results of the annual audit to be conducted by independent
public accountants, pursuant to Section 95 of the New York
City Charter and shall perform such other functions as are
agreed to by all of the members thereof.
Section 5.
Effective Date.
This Order shall take
effect immediately.
