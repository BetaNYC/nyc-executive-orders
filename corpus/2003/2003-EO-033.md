EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 33

APRIL 29, 2003

PURCHASE AND USE OF MOTOR OIL

WHEREAS, environmental protection and energy conservation are promoted by
the appropriate use of re-refined and synthetic motor oil in lieu of virgin motor oil; and
WHEREAS, the Department of Citywide Administrative Services has let or
intends to let requirements contracts to supply re-refined, synthetic and virgin motor oil,
and City agencies will, accordingly, be able to purchase pursuant to such contracts any
one or more of such types of motor oil; and
WHEREAS, section 104-a of the General Municipal Law authorizes a price
preference for recycled products;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the powers vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. For purposes of this Order, the following terms shall have the
following meanings:
(a) "Re-refined motor oil" shall mean motor oil that is licensed by the American
Petroleum Institute and (i) contains a base stock consisting of at least seventy percent oil
from which physical and chemical contaminants acquired through use have been
removed or (ii) contains a base stock of at least fifty percent oil from which physical and
chemical contaminants acquired through use have been removed if motor. oil that
contains a base stock of at least seventy percent such oil is not available in sufficient
quantities.
(b) "Synthetic motor oil" shall mean motor oil that is licensed by the American
Petroleum Institute and contains a base stock derived in the aggregate from no more than
thirty percent of Group I, Group II and Group III base stock categories, as designated by
the American Petroleum Institute, provided that the use of such motor oil permits
extended drain intervals.

§ 2. When the price of re-refined or synthetic motor oil is equal to or less than that
of virgin motor oil, City agencies shall purchase and use re-refined or synthetic motor oil
in lieu of virgin motor oil.
§3. When the price of re-refined motor oil exceeds the price of other categories of
motor oil, but not by more than three percent, City agencies shall purchase and use rerefined motor oil.
§4. When entering into contracts for vehicle maintenance requiring the use of
motor oil, City agencies shall, to the extent practicable, ensure that contractors use rerefined or synthetic motor oil, provided that this section shall not be construed to impede
any agency's ability to obtain the highest quality service at the lowest possible price
through a competitive procurement.
§5. Agencies shall not be required to purchase re-refined or synthetic motor oil, or
to enter into a contract for vehicle maintenance, pursuant to sections 2, 3 and 4 of this
Order:
(a) where federal or state funding restrictions preclude implementation of the
requirements of this Order;
(b) where agencies make emergency procurements pursuant to section 315 of the
Charter;
(c) where motor oil is purchased or used for vehicles operated by the police
department or the fire department, or for vehicles used by any other agency that are
specially equipped for emergency response purposes;
(d) where re-refined or synthetic motor oil does not meet applicable industry
standards for quality for the purposes for which the motor oil is to be used, or where an
agency has determined in writing and informed the Office of Contracts that the quality of
re-refined or synthetic motor oil is not equivalent to or better than that of virgin motor oil.
The reasons for such determination shall also be specified in writing; or
(e) where motor oil other than re-refined or synthetic motor oil is purchased
because re-refined or synthetic motor oil is not available for purchase in sufficient
quantities under existing requirements contracts to meet the needs of the purchasing or
contracting agency, provided that any available supplies of re-refined or synthetic motor

oil under such existing contracts shall be purchased and used pursuant to the
requirements of this Order.
§6. This Order shall take effect 60 days after it is signed, and shall apply to
purchases and contracts executed by agencies after its effective date.
