OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 42
July 7, 1998
DEPARTMENT OF THE HOMELESS
WHEREAS, prior to the enactment of Local Law No. 51 for the year 1993,
services for homeless individuals and families were provided. by the Department of Social
Services under the direction of the New York City Commissioner of Social Services pursuant
to the New York Social Services Law and Chapter 24 of the New York City Charter
(“Charter”); and
WHEREAS, Local Law No. 51 for the year 1993 enacted Chapter 24-A of the
Charter, which established the Department of Homeless Services and provided for the
Commissioner of Homeless Services to have the “powers and perform the duties of a
commissioner of social services under the social services law for the purposes of fulfilling his
or her responsibilities under this chapter”, and also enacted Title 21-A of the Administrative
Code, entitled “Department of Homeless Services”; and
WHEREAS, such local law provided for Chapter 24-A of the Charter and Title
91-A of the Administrative Code to expire on July 1, 1995; and

WHEREAS, Local Law No. 61 for the year 1995 amended Local Law No. 51
for the year 1993 to postpone the expiration of Chapter 24-A and Title 21-A until September 6,
1995; and
WHEREAS, Local Law No. 75 for the year 1995 extended the life of Chapter 24-
A and provided for such chapter to expire on July 1, 1998, and renumbered Title 21-A of the
Administrative Code as Chapter 3 of Title 21 of such Code and provided for such chapter to
expire on July 1, 1998; and
WHEREAS, Local Law No. 75 for the year 1995 provided that the Department
of Homeless Services should expire on July 1, 1998 and that “the functions, powers and duties
of the Department of Homeless Services set forth in chapter 24-A of the New York city charter
and chapter 3 of title 21 of the administrative code of the city of New York ... shall devolve
upon the department of social services...”; and
WHEREAS, legislation to further extend Chapter 24-A of the Charter and Chapter
3 of Title 21 of the Administrative Code has yet to be enacted into law; and
WHEREAS, the Mayor is committed to continuing and supporting the structure
that has provided services to homeless individuals and families and has implemented numerous
improvements to such services; and
WHEREAS, the Mayor wishes to prevent any disruption of such services, and to
this end has directed the Commissioner of Homeless Services to continue to administer the
provision of services to homeless individuals and families as of July 1, 1998;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York it is hereby ordered as follows:

Section 1. There shall be within the Department of Social Services a unit for
homeless services, which shall be called the Department of Homeless Services. The unit shall
be headed by a Deputy Commissioner of the Department of Social Services who shall have the
title Commissioner of Homeless Services, and shall report to the Mayor through the Deputy
Mayor designated by the Mayor with regard to all matters relating to this Order. The
Commissioner of Homeless Services shall possess the qualifications of a Deputy Commissioner
of Social Services as established pursuant to the Social Services Law.
Section 2. The Commissioner of Homeless Services, to the maximum extent
consistent with existing law, shall be responsible for:
a. Directing and supervising the management, operations, budget and funding of
services for homeless individuals and families;
b. Working directly with the City’s Office of Management and Budget with
regard to the funding and administration of the City’s budget for services to homeless individuals
and families;
c. Administering all contracts entered into by the Department of Homeless
Services, including contracts entered into prior to the effective date of this Order;
d.
Advising and assisting the Mayor with respect to matters pertaining to
homeless individuals and families; and
e. Performing such other duties as the Mayor may assign.
Section 3. The Department of Homeless Services within the Department of Social
Services shall have the power to continue any business, proceeding or other matter commenced
by the Department of Homeless Services heretofore.
Any provision
in any law, rule,
regulation, contract, grant or other document relating to the Department of Homeless Services
-3-

described in Chapter 24-A of the Charter and Chapter 3 of Title 21 of the Administrative Code
shall apply to the Department of Homeless Services within the Department of Social Services.
Section 4. This order shall take effect immediately and shall be deemed to have
been in full force and effect as of July 1, 1998.
