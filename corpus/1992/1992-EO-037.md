EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 37
MAY 7 1992
MAYOR’S ADVISORY BOARD TO PROMOTE FAIR HOUSING
WHEREAS, fair housing and fair lending is essential to the City’s growth and
prosperity; and
WHEREAS, notwithstanding substantial efforts by the City to combat housing
discrimination, the residents of New York City still encounter barriers which limit equal
access to housing; and
WHEREAS, the Federal Fair Housing Act and State and City human rights
laws prohibit housing discrimination; and
WHEREAS, equal housing opportunity cannot be realized until the principles
of fair housing are fully accepted by the real estate and building industries, appraisal forms,
financial institutions, property owners’ associations, local government officials, and citizens;
and
WHEREAS, in order to bring together all of the diverse segments of the City
to prevent housing discrimination, it is necessary to create an advisory board to promote fair
housing;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New
York it is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
i
vi
There is hereby established an Advisory Board to Promote Fair Housing ("Advisory
Board") as successor to the NYC Fair Housing Task Force.
The Advisory Board shall
report to and be directly accountable to the Mayor.

The Speaker of the City Council shall be requested to appoint
a representative of the City Council to participate in the
Advisory Board.
Section 3.
Functions of Advisory Board
The Advisory Board shall advise the Mayor with respect to all fair housing issues
affecting the City and shall:
a.
formulate and recommend policies and programs to further
equal housing opportunity;
foster coordination among City, state and federal agencies, and
other organizations and institutions, both public and private,
with respect to fair housing activities;
promote public awareness and education relating to fair housing
and
make
recommendations
regarding the
provision of
information and referral services, and procedures for the speedy
resolution of complaints concerning fair housing;

3.

e.
assist the City Commission on Human Rights in promoting the
enforcement of fair housing laws;
f.
promote
volunteer
assistance
so

g.
appoint a Resource Council to assist the Advisory Board in the
design and implementation of a fair housing program.
The
Resource Council shall include representatives from the Office
of Management and Budget, the Law Department, and the City
Planning Commission, as well as persons in the private and notfor-profit sectors who possess expertise in promoting fair
housing.
The members of the Resource Council shall serve
without compensation and at the pleasure of the Advisory
Board;
h.
compile data and statistics regarding fair housing in New York
City and submit its findings to the Mayor; and
i.
perform such other functions as the Mayor may direct.
Section 4.
The heads of all City departments and agencies shall cooperate with
and assist the Advisory Board.
Section 5,
This Order shall take effect immediately.
