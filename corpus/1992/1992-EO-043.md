EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 43
DECEMBER 2, 1992
THE MAYOR’S OFFICE FOR HOMELESS FACILITIES
AND SERVICE DEVELOPMENT
WHEREAS, over the last three years, this administration has allocated
extensive resources towards addressing the needs of homeless families and individuals in the
City of New York, including the provision of permanent housing to 15,000 homeless families
and quadrupling the number of transitional housing units, and
WHEREAS, in furtherance of this commitment and consistent with
recommendations made by the New York City Commission on Homelessness established
by the Mayor, and in an effort to provide assistance to the homeless in the most effective
and efficient manner, the Mayor will propose legislation to create a new agency whose
function will be to provide City services to homeless families and individuals, expedite the
construction of transitional housing for the homeless, and transfer the operation of such
housing to not-for-profit organizations, and
WHEREAS, the creation of this new City agency will require extensive
planning and coordination with various City, State and federal entities as well as the
enactment of a local law, and

-2-
WHEREAS, in order to begin immediately the improvement of the
development of facilities, and provision of services for homeless families and individuals and
to plan for and oversee the transition to the new agency, it is necessary to establish an
interim Mayoral Office;
NOW, THEREFORE, by virtue of the power vested in me as Mayor of the
City of New York, it is hereby ordered;
Section 1.
Office for Homeless Facilities and Service Development. There
is hereby established in the Office of the Mayor the Office for Homeless Facilities and
Service Development which shall be headed by a Director who shall be appointed by and
serve at the pleasure of the Mayor. The Office shall report to and be directly accountable
to the Deputy Mayor for Health and Human Services.
Section 2.
Responsibilities
of the Office. Until such time as the new agency
is created, the Office shall, in consultation with the Human Resources Administration
(HRA), the Department of Housing Preservation and Development and other agencies
involved in the provision of services for homeless families and individuals, and consistent
with law, (1) oversee and direct on behalf of the Mayor the actions necessary for the
establishment of a new City agency to manage the delivery of services to homeless families
and individuals, (2) participate in the planning and implementation of the redesigning and
restructuring of the current system for the provision of services and facilities for homeless
families and individuals, (3) make recommendations to HRA concerning the planning and
development of and contracting for services and facilities for homeless families and
individuals, and (4) encourage the participation of and receive proposals from the public and

-3the private sectors for the development of services and facilities for homeless families and
individuals, including the evaluation and negotiation of such proposals and the obtaining of
the necessary approvals therefor.
Section 3,
‘In carrying out its responsibilities, the Office shall have the
following functions:
(1) to oversee the planning, design and siting of all new facilities for homeless
families and individuals;
(2)
to make recommendations to and assist HRA in the development of
all new contracts for the provision of services and facilities for the homeless, including
drafting requests for proposals, evaluating the responses thereto, drafting and negotiating
contratts with providers, administering contracts and monitoring performance under the
contracts;
(3)
to participate in the development and coordination of City policy
related to homelessness, in consultation with the Mayor’s Office on Homelessness and Single
Room Occupancy (SRO) Housing Services;;
(4)
to assist in the development of programs designed to improve access
of homeless families and individuals to existing private sector housing;
(5)
to encourage the participation of the private sector in the development
of safe and affordable transitional housing for homeless families and individuals; and
(6)
to facilitate the acquisition, rehabilitation and construction of
transitional housing for homeless families and individuals.
