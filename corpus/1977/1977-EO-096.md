DESIGNATION OF THE EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICE AS THE
COORDINATING AGENCY FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL CARE FOR
THE CITY OF NEW YORK
Whereas, The Emergency Medical Service of the New York City Health and Hospitals
Corporation was established to provide pre-hospital emergency services; and
Whereas, The Emergency Medical Service is the primary provider of pre-hospital emergency medical care in New York City, and is charged with providing safe, expeditious transportation to the medical facility best equipped to render appropriate care, without regard. to any
patient's ability to pay ; and

Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as the Mayor of The City of New York, it is

Section |.
Designation of Coordinating Agency
The Emergency Medical Service of The New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation
ishall be the coordinating agency for The City of New York for all pre-hospital services in the
event of medical emergencies.
§2.
Duties of the Emergency Medical Service
The duties of the Emergency Medical Service shall include:
(a) the operation of ambulance services and funding of ambulances to be operated
under contract ;
(b) the operation of a central dispatching system which shall dircct all responses to
requests for emergency ambulance services and which shall incorpordte all ambulances
operated or funded by The City of New York or the New York City Health and Hospitals
Corporation, as well as such other voluntary and proprietary ambulances as shall choose
to participate and be accepted for participation by the Emergency Medical Service;
(c) the establishment of programs for assuring
the provision of high quality treatment,
administered by qualified personnel, at the scene of
a medical emergency;
(d) the establishment and direction of plans for the provision of transportation services
and vital on-site care in the event of a medical disaster ;
(e) such other and further duties and responsibilities necessary to the proper functioning of the Emergency Medical Service.
§3.
Duties of the Board of Health
Nothing herein shall abridge the authorities and duties of the Board of Health in the event
of great and imminent peril to the public health as specified in Chapter 22, Section 563 of the
New York City Charter.
§4.
Effective Date
This Order shall take effect immediately.
