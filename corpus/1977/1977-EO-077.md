OFFICE OF THE MAYOR —
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 77
,
FEBRUARY 11, 1977
DESIGNATION OF BUSINESS SERVICE LIAISONS
Whereas, It is essential to the economic well-being of the City to promote and encourage the utilization by the business community of the “One-Stop-Business-Service” in
the Office of Economic Development; and
.
Whereas, This vital service assists such businessess in their dealings with City agen- .
cies; an
Whereas, The enhancement and protection of the economic life of the City requires the
full cooperation of all City agencies having dealings with the business community ; and
Whereas, in order to promote. and advance commercial and industrial prosperity in the
City
and to eliminate delays, burdens and other procedures that are having an adverse effect
on
business, it is essential that the One-Stop-Business-Service receive the highest degree of
cooperation from all City agencies.
Now, therefore, by the powers vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it is
hereby ordered as follows:
Section 1.
The One-Stop-Business-Service of the New York City Office of Economic
Development shall function as coordinator for The City of New York on behalf of the
business community to expedite assistance to individual firms in eliminating impediments,
restrictions and delays which cause economic loss,
.
To insure cooperation for the One-Stop-Business-Service with City agencies, the
head of each department shall designate a high-ranking representative with authority to
implement the objectives of this Executive Order and who shall serve as the business service
liaison with the One-Stop-Business-Service. Such designations must be filed in my office and
the Office of the Deputy Mayor for Economic Development within one week of the date of
this
Order,
§3.
The business service liaison from each agency shall:
(a) Refer any specific or general business or business-related problem to the
One-Stop-Business-Service for appropriate action;
) Work directly with the One-Stop-Business-Service to resolye
problems
involving the Department's procedures dnd operations which may adversely affect.
the business community ;
(c) Provide information to the Office of Economic Development to enable it to
evaluate any proposed actions relating to business and make recommendations as to
the economic impact of propriety of taking those actions;
(d)
Keep the Office of Economic Development informed of potential regulatory,
legislative or administrative changes at all levels of government affecting
businesses,
in order to enable the Office of Economic Development to have appropriate input in
the development of policy statements, testimony, reports, etc.
§4. The
Deputy Mayor/Administrator of Economic Development Administration or
his designee shall coordinate and supervise the activities of the One-Stop-Business- Service
and the Business service liaisons.
85.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
