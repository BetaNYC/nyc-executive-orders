OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 56
APRIL 2, 1976
CONTROL OF OVERTIME AND PART-TIME HOURS
Whereas, The City of New York is currently in a fiscal crisis which mandates economies
in every area of fovernmental operations and expenditures: and
Whereas,
The payment of
overtime to employees is an expenditure which must be
curtailed in order to achieve necessary economies rand
Whereas, Situations could arise in which inordinate amounts of overtime paid to employees in their final year of service totally
disproportionate to that received in prior years,
would result in severely inflated costs to The City of New York; and
Whereas, Part-time employees who are eligible to receive pension benefits upon retirement from service could, by working an inordinate number of hours immediately prior to
retirement, seriously inflate costs for The City of
New York; and
Whereas, It is necessary and desirable to control any abuses which might arise in these
areas.
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it is
hereby ordered as follows :
