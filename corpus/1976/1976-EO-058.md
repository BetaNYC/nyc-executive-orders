OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 58
MAY 6, 1976
OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH PLANNING TASK FORCE
Whereas, The City of New York, as an employer, is concerned with providing a safe
cand healthful working environment for its employees and with preventing personal injuries
‘to its employees ; and
Whereas, Federal Jegislation involves the City in various occupational safety and health
requirements ; and
+
Whereas, A City-wide effort is required to assure consistency in rules, regulations and
contracts and compliance with federal, state and local laws; and
Whereas, The New York State Industrial Commissioner has been directed by the
Legislature to determine the types of coverages and the specific occupational safety and
health requirements needed for public employees and to develop and recommend a specific
plan; an
Whereas, A central management team is necessary to efficiently and adequately work
with the State Department of Labor.
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered as follows :
Section 1.
An Occupational Safety and Health Planning Task Force is hereby created
consisting
of the Director of Construction who shall serve as Chairman, the Commissioner
of Health, the Director of Management and Budget, the Director of the Department of
Personnel, the Corporation Counsel and the Director of the Office of Labor Relations, each
of whom may, in writing, designate a representative to serve on the task force in his stead.
§2,
The duties of the task force shall be:
(a) to act as the City’s liaison with the State Department of Labor in safety
and health matters, with particular reference to chapter seyen hundred fifty-six of the
laws of nineteen hundred seventy-five or any subsequent bills or laws on the subject of
public employee occupational safety and health ;
(b) to conduct and supervise a survey of current safety and health rules, regulations, conditions and needs of the City’s employees and
{c) to submit recommendations to the Mayor for changes, improved procedures,
regulations and legislation in the area of public employee safety and health.
Ali agencies shall extend their full cooperation to the task force to the extent
that required personnel shall be reassigned by stich agencies temporarily to assist the task
force in performing
its duties. Each agency also shall designate a staff member at policy
level to serve as liaison with the task force and to cooperate in expeditiously furnishing
such information, data, reports, surveys, studies, etc., and such personnel and resources as
may be requested by the Chairman.
.
All agencies shall cooperate and comply fully with the directives issued by the
Occupational Safety and Health Planning Task Force.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
