OFFICE OF THE MAYOR —
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 70
SEPTEMBER 15, 1976
ESTABLISHMENT OF THE
FINANCIAL INFORMATION SERVICES AGENCY
Whereas, The present financial crisis has highlighted the need to provide certain officers,
employees, departments and agencies of the City with efficient and rapid access to accurate
and current financial information ; and
Whereas, The City requires the prcmpt implementation of a complete restructuring of
its accounting and budgeting
practices and procedures to comply with the provisions of the
New York State Financial Emergency Act for The City
of New York, the Municipal Assistance Corporation for The City of New York Act, the City Charter, as amended by the
electors ata
general election held in November, 1975, certain Accounting Systems Directives
of the State Comptroller and a certain Credit Agreement by and among the City, the United
States of America, the State of New York and the Emergency Financial Control Board; and
Whereas, To accomplish this, the Mayor and the Comptroller have agreed to act together
and under their joint supervision, the Integrated Financial Management System Project
(IFMS) has been started under the co-direction of their respective representatives; and
Whereas, Such implementation and restructuring requires, in addition to IFMS, the
establishment of a single data center for organizing, compiling and coordinating of the City’s
financial records and providing for appropriate use of and access to such information, and
to provide for the management and direction of such center,
Now, therefore, by the power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New York, it is
hereby ordered as follows:
