EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 71
SEPTEMBER 16, 1976
DELEGATION OF AUTHORITY TO DEPUTY MAYOR FOR FINANCE
Whereas, The City of New York may
from time to time enter into agreement with
the Municipal Assistance Corporation for
The City of New York; and
Whereas, Such agreements are beneficial and necessary to the financial stability of
The City of New York: and
Whereas, John C. Burton, Deputy Mayor for Finance of The City of New York, is
fully famillar with the facts necessary to entering such agreements.
Now, therefore, by virtue of the
power vested in me as Mayor of The City of New
York and pursuant to Section three of the New York City Charter, it is hereby ordered
as follows:
Section 1.
John C. Burton, Deputy Mayor for Finance of The City of New
York is hereby delegated the authority to act for, on behalf of, and at the direction
of the Mayor of The City of New York in the exercise of all functions, powers and
duties which the Mayor of The City of New York may have pursuant to Article 10
of the Public Authorities Law as enacted by Laws of 1975, chapters 168 and 169
(the “Act"}, as the same may be amended from time to time, including, but not
limited to, full
power to execute for, on behalf of, and at the direction of the Mayor
of The City of
New York: any agreement pursuant to section 3038 of the Act hetween
The City of New York and the Municipal Assistance Corporation for The City of
New York, any certificate pursuant to section 3037 of the Act and any other document
in connection with the Act.
§2.
The exercise of functions, powers and duties for, on behalf of, and at the
direction of the Mayor of The City of New York by the Deputy Mayor for Finance
of The City of New York pursuant to the delegation established by this Executive
Order, shall not require the consent in writing of the Mayor or any other Deputy
Mayor of The City of New York.
For the
purposes of Article 2, section 9 of the Public Officers Law, as
enacted by Laws of 1909, chapter 51, notwithstanding the temporary inability of the
Mayor of The City of New Vork to exercise such functions, powers and
duties as
he may have pursuant to the Act, by reason of sickness or otherwise, or his absence
from the City, the delegation established by this Executive Order shall devolve
upon the Deputy Mayor jor Finance of The City of New York: and in case of the
concurrent temporary inability
of the Deputy Mayor for Finance of The City of
New York to exercise such
functions, powers and duties, by reason of sickness or
otherwise, or his temporary absence from the City, upon the First Deputy Mayor of
The City of New York.
§4.
Executive Order No. 4) dated September 30, 1975, is hereby revoked,
provided, however, that nothing in this Executive Order shall be construed to affect
the “validity of any act performed pursuant to such Executive Order and prior to the
ate hereal,
§5.
This Executive Order shall take effect immediately and remain in effect
until revoked by a subsequent Executive Order.

ABRAHAM D. BEAME, Mayor.
