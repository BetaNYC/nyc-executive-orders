OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 13
MAY 9, 1978
COUNCIL ON THE ENVIRONMENT
BY VIRTUE OF THE POWER VESTED IN ME AS MAYOR OF THE CITY OF NEW
York, itis hereby ordered:
Section
1.
There is hereby continued in the Office of the Mayor the Council on the Environmenf fof New York City (hereinafter called the Council).
The Council shall promote
environmental concern among New Yorkers and develop solutions to the environmental problems of the City.
2.
The Council shall be composed of not less than 1$ or more than 35 members. Members
shall be appointed by the Mayor for a term of two years. A vacancy occurring other than by
expiration of a term shall be filled by the Mayor for the remainder of the term. Members of
the Council serving presently shall continue to serve until the expiration of their respective
terms.
§3.
The membership of the Council shall include representatives of agencies concerned
with protecting the environment. At least three-quarters of the members shall be individuals
who do not, at the time of their appointment, hold public office or employment. The members
of the Council shall serve without. compensation.
.
The Chairman of the Council shall be designated by the Mayor from among members
of the Council and shall be a resident of the City of New York.
§5.
The duties and powers of the Council shall include the following :
(a) to confer with appropriate City, State, Federal and private agencies concerned |
with protecting the environment for the purpose of considering, developing
and improving
environmental protection progratns and policies and for the purpose of
securing funds
to support the activities of the
Council ;
(b) to conduct research and to operate programs for pollution control and other
environmental protection purposes ;
(c) to sponsor conferences and to prepare and publish reports ;
(d) to encourage joint activities among the various agencies concerned with the protection of the environment ; and
(e) to advise the Mayor on matters concerning the protection of the environment and
to perform such other functions as the Mayor may direct,
§6. City agencies responsible for matters concerning the protection of the environment
shall cooperate with the Council with respect to its function under this Order.
-
The Council shall submit an annual report of its activities to the Mayor.
No expense shall be incurred by the Council for personnel, equipment or otherwise, ;
unless funds sufficient to pay for such expenses have been previously obtained from sources
other than City tax levy funds.
.
Executive Order No. 57 of 1972 is hereby revoked.
10.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
