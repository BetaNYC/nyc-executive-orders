EMERGENCY EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 004
EXTENSION OF EMERGENCY ORDER REGARDING NUSIANCE ABATEMENT
THROUGH DEBRIS REMOVAL
January 6, 2014
WHEREAS, Executive Order 460, issued December 22, 2013, contains a
proclamation extending a state of emergency in the City of New York, and such
proclamation remains in effect for a period not to exceed thirty days or until rescinded,
whichever occurs first; and
WHEREAS, the President of the United States signed a Major Disaster
Declaration under the Stafford Act on October 30, 2012;
WHEREAS, the Governor of the State of New York has issued Executive
Order No. 63, dated Nov. 5, 2012, entitled Declaring Certain Conditions Caused by
Hurricane Sandy in and Around Federally Declared Counties to be Public Nuisances
Impacting Public Health and Ordering the Removal of Those Conditions, which included
determination that debris poses a persistent threat to the security, life and health of
persons in those areas;
WHEREAS, Executive Order 235, issued February 6, 2013, contains an
emergency order regarding nuisance abatement through debris removal;
NOW THEREFORE, it is hereby ordered:

§ 1. The emergency order regarding nuisance abatement through debris removal set forth
in Executive Order 235 is hereby extended for five days.
§ 2. This order shall take effect immediately and shall remain in effect for five (5) days
