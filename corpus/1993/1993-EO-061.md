EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 61
DECEMBER 22 1993
HARLEM COMMUNITY EMPOWERMENT ZONE
WHEREAS, Subchapter C of Title XIII of the Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act
of 1993, Public Law 103-66 (the "Act”) authorizes the establishment of an Empowerment
Zone Program under which a community of not more than 200,000 people in New York City
may be designated to receive special investment and training credits and major funding for
social services which would enhance the economic self-sufficiency of zone residents; and
WHEREAS, the Act allows an area to be nominated for Empowerment Zone status
by the local government and State in which it is located or by an economic development
corporation chartered by the State; and
WHEREAS, the community of Harlem, defined as the areas of Community Boards
9, 10, and 11, meets the eligibility criteria for designation as an Empowerment Zone under
the Act; and
WHEREAS, Rep. Charles B. Rangel of the Fifteenth Congressional District has
invited the City of New York to work with him in securing Empowerment Zone status for
the Harlem Community; and
WHEREAS, the Harlem Urban Development Corporation ("“HUDC’), a state
chartered economic development corporation, has expressed a strong interest in nominating
Harlem for Empowerment Zone status; and
WHEREAS, the Act mandates that the affected community be a full partner in the
strategic planning process leading to submission of an application for designation of
Empowerment Zone status;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York,
it is hereby ordered as follows:

-2-
Section 1.
Strategic Planning and Application Process,
a.
The City and HUDC shall cooperate in the preparation of an
application for designation of the Harlem community as an Empowerment Zone to be
submitted by the City and the State to the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban
Development.
b.
HUDC shall coordinate the strategic planning process mandated
by the Act, in order to ensure that such process is open to participation by the entire
Harlem community and reflects the views of Harlem residents, representatives of the
Harlem business community, and organizations concerned with the economic development
and social service needs of the area. The strategic planning process coordinated by HUDC
shall include the conduct of public hearings, the solicitation of written proposals, and other
outreach effects.
Section 2.
Harlem Community Empowerment Zone Advisory Committee.
An advisory committee appointed by the Mayor in consultation with
HUDC shall be established to assist the HUDC and the City in developing implementation
strategies under the strategic plan. The Advisory Committee shall include representatives
of the State Department of Economic Development, the business community, foundations,
the steering committee of the Harlem Economic Development Initiative, and other
appropriate social service and economic development entities.
