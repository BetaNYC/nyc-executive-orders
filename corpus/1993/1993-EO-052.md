THE DEVELOPMENT OF RECYCLING MARKETS AND BUSINESSES
WHEREAS, it is critical to the successful implementation of the City’s solid
waste management plan, the cornerstone of which is an aggressive recycling program, that
sufficient processing capacity and markets exist for the recyclable materials collected by the
City and private companies; and
WHEREAS, businesses associated with the re-manufacture of recyclable
materials are potentially a significant source of capital investment and new jobs in the City.
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as the Mayor of the City
of New York, it is hereby ordered as follows:
Section 1.
Interagency Task Force
a.
There is hereby established an Interagency Task Force on the
Development of Recycling Markets and Businesses. The Task Force shall report to the
Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development.
b.
The Task Force shall be chaired by the Commissioner of the
Department of Sanitation, or his or her designee.
The Deputy Mayor for Finance and
Economic Development shall request the President of the Economic Development
Corporation, or his or her designee, to act as co-chair.
c.
The following City agencies and divisions of the Office of the Mayor
shall participate on the Task Force: the Department of General Services, the Department
of Environmental Protection, the Law Department, the Department of Employment, the
Office of the Deputy Mayor for Planning and Development, the Mayor’s Office of
Operations, the Mayor’s Office of Management and Budget, the Mayor’s Office of Contracts,
the Mayor’s Office of City Legislative Affairs, and the Office of Economic Planning and
Marketing. The head of each participating entity shall designate a senior official to serve
on the Task Force.

-2.
Section 2.
Functions
of
k Fore
The Task Force shall be responsible for formulating a coordinated
Strategy to encourage the growth of markets for the City’s recyclables and to attract recycling
businesses to New York City, working in consultation with the Mayor’s Advisory Council on
the Development of Recycling Markets and Businesses established under Section 3 of this
Order. The Task Force shall develop a strategy based upon:
a.
an assessment of the most effective methods of marketing the City’s
recyclable materials;
b.
an identification of those recycling industries most suited to
New York City;
c.
an evaluation of the economic incentives that would be most
effective in attracting recycling businesses to the City and
expanding existing businesses in the City; and
d.
an analysis of methods for increasing the City’s procurement of
products with recycled content.
Section 3.
Advisory Council
a.
There is hereby established a Mayor’s Advisory Council on the
Development of Recycling Markets and Businesses. The Advisory Council shall include
representatives of the business community, environmental groups, and other organizations
having recycling expertise. The members shall be appointed for a term of two years and
shall serve without compensation. The City Council shall also be requested to participate
on the Advisory Council.
b.
The Advisory Council shall be chaired by the Commissioner of the
Department of Sanitation, or his or her designee.
The Deputy Mayor for Finance and
Economie Development shall request the President of the Economic Development
Corporation, or his or her designee, to act as co-chair.
c.
The Advisory Council shall assist the Mayor and the Task Force on the
Development of Recycling Markets and Businesses in formulating the City’s coordinated
Strategy to encourage the growth of markets for the City’s recyclables and to attract recycling
businesses to New York City.

| This Order shall take effect immediately.
