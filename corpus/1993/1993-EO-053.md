THE City oF New YoRK
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 53
January 29, 1993
DEPUTY MAYORS AND COUNSEL TO THE MAYOR
(Supersedes Executive Order No. 33, dated 12/27/91)
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section 1. There shall be six Deputy Mayors. One shall be designated the First
Deputy Mayor, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic
Development, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Planning and Development, one
shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Public and Community Affairs, one shall be
designated Deputy Mayor for Public Safety, and one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for
Health and Human Services.
§2.
a.
The First Deputy Mayor shall:
Supervise and coordinate the operations of all administrations, departments,
agencies, boards, offices, committees, and commissions except as otherwise
provided herein.
Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Mayor’s Office of Operations,
the Office of City Legislative Affairs, the Office of State Legislative Affairs,
and the Mayor’s Office in Washington, D.C.
Act on behalf of the Mayor, either directly or through the Office of City
Legislative Affairs, the Office of State Legislative Affairs, or the Mayor’s
Office in Washington, D.C., with respect to all City, State and federal
legislative matters affecting the City; direct the activities of the Mayor’s
legislative representatives in New York City, Albany, and Washington, D.C.;
represent the Mayor before City Council, Congressional and State legislative
committees; and maintain liaison with the Office of the President and all
federal departments and agencies, the Office of the Governor and all State
departments and agencies, and the City Council.

2.
Serve as the Mayor’s representative, either directly or through the Offices of
City and State Legislative Affairs, on, and maintain liaison with, regional
bodies and intergovernmental agencies operating within the New York
metropolitan region.
Maintain liaison, either directly or through the Mayor’s Office in Washington,
D.C., with the United States Conference of Mayors, the National League of
Cities, the New York Conference of Mayors and other similar bodies.
Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Board of Education, the
New York City School Construction Authority, the City University of New
York, and the Metropolitan Transportation Authority.
Supervise and coordinate the preparation of the City’s Revenue, Expense and
Capital Budgets and the operations of the Office of Management and Budget.
Develop, supervise and coordinate policies and programs relating to personnel
and management practices.
Succeed to the powers and duties of the "Deputy Mayor - City Administrator
of the City" under section 7384 of the Unconsolidated Laws (Chapter 1016 of
the Laws of 1969).
Act for and on behalf of the Mayor in the exercise of all functions, powers
and duties which the Mayor may have pursuant to the New York State Local
Finance Law, as the same may from time to time be amended, the New York
State Financial Emergency Act for the City of New York, as the same may
from time to time be amended, Article 10 of the Public Authorities Law, as
the same may from time to time be amended, and the New York City
Municipal Water Finance Authority Act, as the same may from time to time
be amended, including, but not limited to, full power to execute for and on
behalf of the Mayor, any agreement, amendment to such agreement or other
document pursuant to such laws.
Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the Mayor, a certificate
approving any borrowing of funds or pledge of assets or revenues by the New
York City Health and Hospitals Corporation which would require the
approval of the Mayor pursuant to the "Memorandum of Agreement Between
New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation and The City of New York
Relating to Operating Expenditures by the Corporation", dated July 1, 1970.
Execute and deliver, in the name and on behalf of the City, Official
Statements of the City relating to the issuance of the City’s obligations.
