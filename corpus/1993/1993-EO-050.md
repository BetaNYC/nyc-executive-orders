INTEGRATED CONTRACT AND PROCUREMENT MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
WHEREAS, the City desires to more effectively manage and streamline the
contracting and procurement process for necessary goods, services and construction, and to
improve efficiency and cost-effectiveness in procurement; and
WHEREAS, in order to advance these goals it has been determined that a
comprehensive automated contract management system, to be known as the Integrated
Contract and Procurement Management System (the "ICPMS"), should be created; and
WHEREAS, in order to facilitate the operation of the ICPMS, it is desirable to
establish a governing structure to ensure that the ICPMS effectively supports the
procurement function:
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York,
it is hereby ordered that:
Section 1.
a.
There shall be established within the Mayor’s
Office of Contracts an Integrated Contract and
Procurement Management System (known as the
“ICPMS") which shall, to the maximum extent
possible, computerize and automate all aspects of
the contracting and procurement processes of
City agencies.
b.
The Mayor’s Office of Contracts shall
be
responsible for the ICPMS, including operation
and maintenance, personnel and staffing, and all
other activities necessary for the achievement of
its mission.

-2c.
The ICPMS shall provide support for all aspects
of City procurement, and shall have the capacity
to compile and process information regarding
vendor
registration,
solicitation
and
award
processes, vendor selection, contract registration,
contract administration, performance monitoring,
vouchering, payments and any other pertinent
management information concerning the
contracting and procurement activities of City
agencies,
d.
The ICPMS shall also contain an electronic data
interchange component that will permit vendors
to access information on contract opportunities,
download solicitation documents, submit invoices,
and receive payment electronically.
e.
The ICPMS shall be accessible to the public at a
central location to allow review of such standard
information regarding each City contract and
contractors as is required to be available for
public examination pursuant to Charter Section
1064 and the Rules of the Procurement Policy
‘Board.
Section 2.
Established
a.
There is
hereby established
an
Integrated
Contract and Procurement Management System
Policy Board (the "ICPMS Board").
b.
The ICPMS Board shall consist of six members.
Three members shall be appointed by the Mayor,
two shall be appointed by the Comptroller, and
one ex-officio (non-voting) member shall be a
representative of the Procurement Policy Board.
Each member shall serve at the pleasure of his or
her appointing authority.
c.
The Director of the Mayor’s Office of Contracts
shall serve as Chair of the ICPMS Board.
©

-3d.
The ICPMS Board shall be responsible for the
establishment of policy regarding the design,
development and operation of the ICPMS. The
ICPMS Board shall act by majority vote of the
members entitled to vote, except that the vote of
four members entitled to vote shall be necessary
for all
policy
decision
concerning:
(i)
the
collection, utilization and reporting of data on
City contracts and contractors; (ii) the payment
process, and other financial transactions; (iii)
linkages with any other relevant automated
information
systems;
and
(iv)
the
internal
management controls of the ICPMS.
e.
The ICPMS Board, acting in ‘consultation with
the Procurement Policy Board as appropriate,
shall promulgate such guidelines and procedures
as may be necessary and appropriate to effectuate
the purposes of this Order.
Section 3.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
