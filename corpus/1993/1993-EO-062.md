EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 62
DECEMBER 23, 1993
NEW YORK CITY COMMISSION AND MAYOR’S TASK FORCE ON THE
REDEVELOPMENT OF NAVAL STATION NEW YORK
WHEREAS, despite substantial efforts by the City and its Congressional delegation
to keep Naval Station New York (the "Homeport") at Staten Island, the United States
government has indicated that it intends to close the Homeport; and
WHEREAS, the City is now faced with the challenge of finding an alternative use
for the Homeport in order to redress the impact of the base closure and provide new
economic development benefits to the City; and
WHEREAS, a careful assessment of the various alternatives is necessary in order to
develop a plan to utilize the Homeport site and its facilities to achieve their potential; and
WHEREAS, development of a plan can best be achieved by involving community and
business leaders in the process;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York,
it is hereby ordered as follows:
Section 1.
New York City Commission on the Redevelopment of Naval Station
New_York,
a.
Establishment of Commission, There is hereby established the New
York City Commission on the Redevelopment of Naval Station New York (the
“Commission"), The Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development or his or her
designee shall act as liaison with the Commission.

-2b.
Membership of Commission, The Members of the Commission shall
include business leaders, representatives of cultural, civic, and social service organizations,
and individuals residing in the community in which the Homeport site is located.
The
Mayor shall designate the Chair of the Commission.
C.
_ Functions of Commission, The functions and duties of the Commission
shall be:
(i)
in consultation with the Task Force hereinafter
established, to assess various alternatives and
recommendations for reuse of the Homeport site
and the facilities thereon;
(ii)
to develop and recommend to the Task Force
and the Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic
Development a plan to utilize the Homeport site
and the facilities thereon to their full potential;
and
(iii) to perform such other related functions as the Mayor may direct.
