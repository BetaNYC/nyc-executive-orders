November 29 1993
THE MAYOR’S DOMESTIC VIOLENCE COORDINATING COUNCIL
(Executive Order No. 57, dated 10/21/93, Repealed)
WHEREAS, it is estimated that domestic violence affects as many as 279, 300
households in New York City every year; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence is a devastating phenomena that respects no
boundaries of race, ethnicity, age, gender, sexual orientation, or economic status; and
WHEREAS, the policies and programs of this Administration have been dedicated
to assisting children and families in need; and
WHEREAS, during the past four years the City has more than doubled the number
of emergency shelter beds for victims of domestic violence and their children, and has for
the first time ever designed non-residential services for this population; and
WHEREAS, the City has incorporated domestic violence prevention into the New
York City Police Department’s community policing program, thereby expanding the "Safe
Streets, Safe City" program, into a "Safe Streets, Safe Homes" program; and
WHEREAS, the development of an integrated approach to the problem of domestic
violence and the coordination of existing systems and services are critical to the success of
the City’s efforts in this area;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York,
it is hereby ordered;
Section 1.
Prior Order Repealed, Executive Order No. 57 of 1993, dated October
21, 1993, is hereby repealed.

Section 2,
Establishment
of a Coordinating Council. There is hereby established
a Mayor’s Domestic Violence Coordinating Council (the “Coordinating Council"),
The
Director of the Mayor’s Office for Children and Families or his or her designee shall serve
as chairperson of the Coordinating Council and shall coordinate and supervise its activities.
The Coordinating Council shall report to the Deputy Mayor for Health and Human
Services, the Deputy Mayor for. Planning and Development, and the Deputy Mayor for
Public Safety.

Section 3.
Purpose. The Coordinating Council shall:
a.
Formulate policies and programs relating to all aspects of services and
protocols for victims of domestic violence;
b.
Develop methods to improve the coordination of systems and services
for victims of domestic violence;
Cc.
Create mechanism to ensure that each affected city agency responds
appropriately to domestic violence situations and that there is coordination among the
agencies involved; and
d.
Launch public education campaign to heighten awareness of domestic
violence and its devastating effects on society.
Section 4.
Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately.
