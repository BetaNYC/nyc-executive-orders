THE City OF NEw YoRK
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 51
JANUARY 25 1993
HIV HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES PLANNING COUNCIL
(Amendment of Executive Order No. 23, dated 1/18/91)
By the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is hereby
ordered as follows:
Section 1. Prior Order Amended. Section 3 of Executive Order No. 23, dated
January 18, 1991, as amended by Executive Order No. 36, dated February 5,
1992, is hereby amended to read as follows:
Section 3. Membership.
(a) The Planning Council
shall be appointed by the Mayor and shall consist of
not less than 35 and not more than 45 members,
exclusive of those non-voting, ex officio members
appointed pursuant to subsection (i). The members
of the Planning Council and any alternates that are
designated to serve as voting participants of the
Planning Council pursuant to subsection (g) shall
serve at the pleasure of the Mayor. From among the
members of the Planning Council, the Mayor shall
designate a Chairperson.
(b)
Each. member of the Planning Council other
than those persons enumerated in subsections (e), (f)
and (i) shall be appointed for two year terms, except
that of the members first appointed, half shall be
appointed for terms of one year and half shall be
appointed for terms of two years. No such member

shall serve for more than two consecutive two year
terms.
(c) The membership of the Planning Council shall
include representatives of health care providers,
community-based and AIDS service organizations,
social
service
providers,
mental
health
care
providers, local public health agencies, hospital
planning agencies or health care planning agencies,
affected communities (including individuals with
HIV/AIDS), non-clected community leaders, State
government, grantees of categorical grants for early
intervention services under subpart II of part C of
the Ryan White Act and the lead agency of any
Health Resources and Services Admini-stration adult
and pediatric HIV-related care demonstration project
operating in the area to be served.
At least one
member of the Planning Council shall represent
separately each of these specified categories.
(d)
Twenty-five (25) percent of the positions on
the Planning Council shall be allocated to individuals
with HIV/AIDS.
(e)
The Mayor also shall appoint one member
that is mutually acceptable to the Mayor and the
County Executives of Westchester, Rockland and
Putnam
Counties
to
represent
these
political
subdivisions or Health Resources and Services
Administration grant recipients therein on the
Planning Council.
(f)
The Planning Council shall also include as
members the Commissioner of Mental Health,
Mental Retardation, and Alcoholism Services of the
City of New York, the Commissioner of Health of
the City of New York, the Administrator of the New
York City Human Resources Administration, the
Director of the Office of Management and Budget of
the City of New York, and the President of the
Health and Hospital Corporation.
(g)
| All members of the Planning Council may
designate an alternate to serve as a voting participant

in the Planning Council on the occasion of the
member's
absence.
Such alternates
must
be
approved by the Chairperson of the Planning
Council.
(h)
There must be at least one member of the
Planning Council from each of New York City’s
boroughs.
(i)
The following shall serve as non-voting, ex
officio members of the Planning Council: Director
of Mayor’s Office on Special Needs Housing,
Director of Mayor’s Office on Drug Abuse Policy,
Chairperson of the City Human Rights Commission,
Commissioner of Aging of the City of New York,
Director of Mayor’s Office of Operations, Director
of Mayor’s Office for People with Disabilities, and
Director of Mayor’s Office on Homelessness and
Single Room Occupancy Housing Services.
(j)
In the event of a vacancy, the Planning
Council may within thirty (30) days after the
vacancy occurs recommend to the Mayor the names
of three individuals who would satisfy the relevant
qualifying criteria to fill that vacancy.
Section 2.
Executive Order No. 23, dated January 18, 1991, is amended by
renumbering Sections 6 and 7 thereof as Sections 8 and 9, respectively, and
adding new Sections 6 and 7 to read as follows:
Section 6. Reporting Requirements. The Planning
Council shall
report all of its decisions and
recommendations to the Mayor through the Deputy
Mayor for Health and Human Services. In addition,
the Planning Council shall submit an annual report
to the Mayor on the delivery of services under Title
I of the Ryan White Act.
Such report shall also
contain the Planning Council’s recommendations
regarding the delivery of such services under Title
I of the Ryan White Act as well as the delivery of
other HIV/AIDS services
needed
to effect a
comprehensive continuum of care for persons
impacted by the HIV/AIDS epidemic.

Section 7.
Staff.
The Planning Council may
recommend and shall be provided with such paid
staff members as are approved by the Mayor.
Section 3. Effective Date. This Order shall take effect immediately.
DAVID N. DINKINS
MAYOR
