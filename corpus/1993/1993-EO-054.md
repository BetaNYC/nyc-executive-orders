EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 54
March 5 , 1993
BUSINESS EMERGENCY TASK FORCE
WHEREAS, the economic well-being of New York City is
dependent, in part, upon the City's ability to respond to the needs
of its businesses and industries in times of emergency; and
WHEREAS, representatives of City agencies involved in the
regulation of and delivery of municipal services to businesses must
be able to communicate quickly and effectively with one another in
times of emergency;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the
City of New York, it is hereby ordered:
Section. 1.
Creation of Task Force.
There is hereby
established the Business Emergency Task Force (the "Task Force").
The Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development or his or her

designee shall serve as a member and as chairperson of the Task
Force and shall coordinate and supervise its activities.
§ 2.
Business Emergency.
For the purposes of this
Executive Order, the term "business emergency" means any occasion
or
instance
for
which,
in
the
determination
of
the
Mayor,
circumstances require special assistance to protect commercial
property
and aid businesses
or
to lessen or avert
damage to
business interests in the City.
§ 3.
Membership.
a.
The
following City officers shall also
be
members of the Task Force:
(1)
the Commissioner of Business Services or his or
her designee;
.
(2)
the Commissioner of Buildings or his or her
designee;
(3)
the Police Commissioner or his or her designee;
and
(4)
the Fire Commissioner or his or her designee.
Any person designated to sit on the Task Force pursuant
to this subdivision shall be a senior official or staff member
having experience in and familiarity with matters pertaining to
“emergency response.

b.
In addition to the above-named officers, the
Mayor shall invite each of the following entities to designate one

§ 4.
Functions of Task Force.
The functions and duties
of the Task Force shall include:
a.
determining what steps should be taken to
protect commercial property and aid businesses or to lessen or
avert damage to business interests in the City in the event of a
business emergency;
b.
determining the availability to businesses in
the City of emergency assistance funds;
c.
notifying members of the business community of
a business emergency or of the possibility thereof;
dq.
in the event that
a business emergency is
determined to exist, coordinating the assignment of appropriate
City personnel to the affected area to assess business related
issues and concerns; and
e.
undertaking such other activities and measures
as may be directed by the Mayor to ensure communication between the
City and the business community during a business emergency.
§ 5.
Effective Date.
This Order shall take effect
immediately.
