INTERAGENCY TASK FORCE ON SMALL BUSINESS
AND
THE SMALL BUSINESS ADVISORY BOARD
WHEREAS, the economic well-being of New York City is dependent, in
part, upon the City’s ability to respond to the needs of its businesses and industries; and
WHEREAS, representatives of City agencies involved in the regulation
of and delivery of municipal services to small businesses must be able to communicate quickly
and effectively with one another in order to ensure productive interaction between the City and
the small business community; and
WHEREAS, the economic vitality of the City’s small business community
is dependent, in part, on the ability of the private sector to communicate with the public sector
on ways to improve the economic vitality of the City’s small business community;
NOW THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City
of New York, it is hereby ordered that Executive Order No. 30 of October 2, 1991, is amended
to read as follows:
Section 1.
Creation of Task Force.
There is hereby established within the
office of the Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development an Interagency Task Force
on Small Business ("Task Force").

§ 3.
Membership.
a.
The following City agencies shall participate in the Task Force: Department
of Buildings; Department of Business Services; Department of City Planning; Department of
Consumer Affairs; Department of Employment; Department of Environmental Protection;
Department of Finance; Fire Department; Department of General Services; Department of
.
Health; Law Department; Police Department; Department of Sanitation; Department of
Telecommunications and Energy; Department of Transportation; the Economic Policy and
Marketing Group; and such other City agencies as the Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic
Development may hereinafter designate.
In addition, the Deputy Mayor for Finance and
Economic Development shall direct the New York City Economic Development Corporation to
participate in the Task Force.
b.
The head of each participating entity shall designate a deputy commissioner
or a senior official occupying a comparable position to serve on the Task Force. The Mayor,
the Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development and the Commissioner of the
Department of Business Services shall be notified of such designation within two weeks of the
date of this order.
§ 4. Coordination. The Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development
or his or her designee shall serve as chairperson of the Task Force and shall coordinate and
supervise its activities.
§ 5.
Creation of Mayor's Small Business Advisory Board.
There is hereby
established the Mayor’s Small Business Advisory Board (the "Advisory Board") to advise the
Mayor and Deputy Mayor for Finance and Economic Development on matters relating to the

§ 6.
Functions of Advisory Board.
The functions and duties of the Advisory
Board shall be limited to:
a.
evaluating the regulatory procedures and operations of City agencies that
affect New York City’s small business community;
b.
making recommendations to the Department of Business Services and the
Task Force with respect to the City agency rules designed to promote and foster the growth of
small businesses in the City;
c. making recommendations to the Department of Business Services and the Task
Force with respect to issues which have a significant impact on the small business community;
and
d.
undertaking other activities and measures to enhance cooperation between the
City and the small business community as directed by the Deputy Mayor for Finance and
Economic Development.
§ 7.
Membership.
Members of the Advisory Board shall be selected by the
Commissioner of Business Services upon consultation with the Deputy Mayor for Finance and
Economic Development. The Advisory Board shall consist of small business owners, including
those persons, firms, corporations, or other enterprises which operate small businesses; industry,
merchants, and trade associations; and other organizations which represent small businesses in
the City. There shall be at least twenty-five and no more than fifty members on the Advisory
Board. Advisory Board members shall serve for a term set by the Deputy Mayor for Finance
and Economic Development.
§ 8.
Executive Order No. 30, dated October 2, 1991, is repealed.
§ 9.
Effective Date.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
