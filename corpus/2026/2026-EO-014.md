THE City oF NEw YORK
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
New York, N.Y. 10007
EXECUTIVE ORDER No. 14
March 13, 2026
OFFICE OF LGBTQIA+ AFFAIRS
WHEREAS, LGBTQIA+ people across the United States are facing an increasingly hostile
political environment; and
WHEREAS, LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers represent at least 15 percent of the City’s
population, comprising a community that intersects and enriches the entire City, yet they have
unique needs that have been historically underfunded and left unaddressed; and
WHEREAS, the cost-of-living crisis confronting the City’s working class hits the
LGBTQIA+ community particularly hard, with higher rates of unemployment and homelessness
than the City population at large; and
WHEREAS, this Administration believes that LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers should be
represented in all aspects of our City; and
WHEREAS, the NYC Unity Project was launched in September 2017 as New York City’s
first Citywide initiative focused on delivering and coordinating affirming services for LGBTQIA+
New Yorkers; and
WHEREAS, this Administration is committed to identifying and addressing the needs of
the LGBTQIA+ community and to providing a single destination for LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers to
turn to for matters affecting their communities;
NOW, THEREFORE, by the power vested in me as Mayor of the City of New York, it is
hereby ordered:
Section 1. There is hereby established an Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs within the Office
of the Mayor. The Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs (“the Office’) shall operate within and report to
the Mayor’s Office of Equity & Racial Justice which shall be supervised and coordinated by the
Deputy Mayor for Economic Justice. The Office shall be headed by a Director appointed by the
Mayor and shall replace the NYC Unity Project, whose former responsibilities will be carried out
pursuant to this Order.

§ 2. The Director of the Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs shall have the power and duty to:
a.
Oversee and coordinate the implementation of initiatives concerning LGBTQIA+
New Yorkers across all City agencies, including community outreach and public
education;
Assist City agencies in developing and supporting liaisons to the LGBTQIA+
community;
Coordinate with the New York City Commission on Human Rights, the
Commission on Gender Equity, and other City entities as appropriate to advance
measures to ensure offices and agencies do not engage in discrimination based on
gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation;
Work with the Mayor’s Office of Nonprofit Services in outreach to nonprofit
organizations serving LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers to increase awareness of the City’s
initiatives and procedures to ensure that nonprofits receive timely dispersal of funds
for City contracts;
In coordination with the Office of the Chief Counsel to the Mayor and City Hall,
the Law Department, the New York City Commission on Human Rights, the
Commission on Gender Equity, and other City entities as appropriate, develop legal
resources to ensure the City’s sanctuary status for LGBTQIA+ individuals is
upheld, providing support for LGBTQIA+ people and their families escaping
oppression for identifying as LGBTQIA+;
Assume any function served by the NYC Unity Project prior to the effective date
of this Order, including, but not limited to, any such function assigned by executive
order or local law, and serve on the Gender and Racial Equity Advisory Board in
place of the former Executive Director of the NYC Unity Project; and
Perform any other duty, responsibility, or function assigned by the Mayor or Deputy
Mayor for Economic Justice.
§ 3. Upon request of the Director, mayoral agencies shall cooperate with and provide
assistance to the Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs to further the performance of its duties.
§ 4. This Order shall take effect immediately.
