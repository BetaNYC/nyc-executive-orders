EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 27
JANUARY 26, 1979
DEPUTY MAYORS
BY THE POWER VESTED IN ME AS MAYOR OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK, IT
is hereby ordered:
Section 1.
There shall be seven Deputy ‘Mayors. One of them shall be designated Deputy
Mayor for Administration, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Intergovernmental Relations, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Policy, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor
for Economic Policy and Development, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Financial
Management, one shall be designated Deputy Mayor for Criminal Justice, and one shall be
designated Deputy Mayor for Human Services.
§2.
The Deputy Mayor for Administration shall:
(a) Develop and supervise programs involving coordination among administrations,
departments, boards and agencies, and including management and personnel practices and
matters arising out of the periodic reports submitted to the Mayor by the heads of agencies,
_
(b>) Administer, on behalf of the Mayor, Executive Order No. 90 with the Director of
Management and Budget.
(c) Represent the Mayor when so directed on any board, committee, commission or
corporation of which the
Mayor is a member.
Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
, $3.
The Deputy Mayor for Intergovernmental Relations shall:
(a) In the absence of the ‘Mayor from sessions of the Board of Estimate act on behalf
of the Mayor at such sessions of the Board of Estimate.
(b) Act on behalf of the Mayor with respect to all Federal, State and local legislative
matters affecting the City; direct the activities of the Mayor’s legislative representative,
the Albany office, and the Washington, D.C. office ; represent the Mayor before Congressional
and State legislative committees, maintain liaison with the Office of the President and all
Federal departments and agencies thereof, the Office of the Governor and all State departments and agencies thereof, the Board of Estimate and the City Council and supervise the
activities of the New York City Commission for the United Nations and for the Consular
arps,
re) Serve as the Mayor’s representative on and maintain liaison with the regional
bodies and intergovernmental agencies operating within the New York metropolitan region,
(d) Maintain liaison with the United States Conference of Mayors, the National
League of Cities, the New York State Conference of Mayors and other similar bodies.
(e) Represent the Mayor when so directed by the Mayor on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
(£) Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§4.
The Deputy Mayor for Policy shall:
(a) Advise and assist the Mayor on matters of City policy.
(b) Maintain liaison with and review the activities of the Board of Education and the
Board of Higher Education of The City of New York.
(c) Represent the Mayor when so directed by the Mayor on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
(d) Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.
§5.
The Deputy Mayor for Economic Policy and Development shall:
(a) Head the City’s economic development program, including tax, energy and government regulatory policy and coordinate the efforts to foster the economic growth of the City.
(b) Direct the activities of the Office for Economic Development and the Business
Marketing Corporation, coordinate the activities of the Public Development Corporation
and the Industrial Development Agency, and act as Chairman of the Development Funds
Steering Committee.
(c) Represent the Mayor when so directed by the Mayor on any board, committee, commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member,
(d) Perform such other duties as the Mayor may direct.

§7.
The Deputy Mayor for Criminal Justice shalt:
(a) Serve as the coordinator of Criminal justice provided by section 13 of the New
York City Charter.
(b) Supervise and coordinate the operations of the Department of Correction, Police
Department and Department of Probation.
(c) Represent the Mayor when so directed by the Mayor on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is a member.
(d) Perform such other duties as the Mayor shall direct.
§8.
The Deputy Mayor for Human Services shall:
(a) Develop and supervise planning and programs among administrations, departments,
boards and agencies in the areas of public assistance, health care, services to the aging and
community action.
(b)
Succeed to the powers and duties of the Health Services Administrator, as specified in Chapter 1016 of the Laws of 1969 (Section 7381 et seq. of the Unconsolidated Laws
of New York).
(c) Represent the Mayor when so directed by the Mayor on any board, committee,
commission or corporation of which the Mayor is
a member.
(d) Perform such other duties as the Mayor shall direct.
