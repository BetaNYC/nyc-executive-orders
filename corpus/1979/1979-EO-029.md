OFFICE OF THE MAYOR

EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 29
FEBRURAY 6, 1979
ESTABLISHMENT OF AN AUDIT COMMITTEE FOR THE CITY OF NEW YORK
BY THE POWER VESTED IN ME AS MAYOR OF THE CITY OF NEW YORE
it is hereby ordered :
‘
Section 1.
Audit Commitice Established. An Audit Committee for the City of New York
(the “Audit Committee’) is hereby established to consist of the Mayor, the Comptroller, the
President of the Council, each of whom shall serve ex officio (the “City
Members”), and four
other persons (the “Private Members’) selected by the New York
State Financial Control
Board (the “Board”) as provided in Section 2 hereof. The members of the Audit Committee
(shall elect a Private Member as chairman of the Audit Committee for an annual term commencing on March 1 of each year.
§2.
Private Members.
The Board will select for
appointment as Private Members of the
Audit Committee four (4) individuals possessing the qualifeations set forth in section 103(7) (B)
of the New York City Loan Guarantee Act of 1978, P.L. 95-339, 31 USC 1521-1531 (the “Act”).
Such section of the Act currently specifies (i) two individuals with expertise in municipal finance and (ii) two officers or employces of two different firms of independent public accountants
which are not engaged either by the City of New York or by the Comptroller of the City. Each
individual so selected shall thereupon be appointed a Private Member by the Mayor and shall
serve for a term of one year commencing March 1; provided, however, that the terms of the
Private Members appointed initially shall cover the period from the dates of their respective
appointments to and including February
29, 1980. Private Members shall serve without salary,
except that each Private Member shall
be entitled to reimbursement for his actual and necessary
expenses incurred in the performance of his official duties as a Private Member of the Audit
Committee and shall also receive a per diem allowance of one hundred dollars ($100) when
rendering services as a Private Member, provided that the aggregate per diem allowance to any
one Private Member in any one annual term of office shail not exceed the sum of one thousand
doilars ($1800).
§ 3.
Functions of the Audit
Committee.
The Audit Committee shall assist in the determination of areas of inquiry for, review the progress of, and evaluate the results of the annual
audit to be conducted by independent public accountants, pursuant to the Agreement to Guarantee made and entered into as of the 15th day of November 1978, by and among the City, the
United States of America, and certain other parties pursuant to the Act and shall perform
such other functions as are agreed to by all of the other members thereof.
Indemnification,
The City of New York shall indemnify and hold harmless each
member of the Audit Committee from financial loss arising out of any claim, demand, suit,
action or judgment for any act or failure to act by such member within the scope of his or her
duties hereunder. Any member of the Audit Committee seeking indemnification shall, within
eight (8) days after the date on which he is served with any summons, complaint, process, notice,
demand, claim or pleading, deliver the original or a true copy thereof to the Corporation Counsel
of the City of New York. Upon such delivery the Corporation Counsel of the City of New York
shall assume contro! of the representation of such member in connection with such claim, demand,
_ suit, action or proceeding. Such member shall cooperate fully with the Corporation Counsel of
| the City of New York or any other person designated to assume such defense in respect of such
representation or defense.
§5.
Effective Date.
This Order shall take effect immediately.
